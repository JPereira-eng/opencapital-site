== TYPOLOGIES COVERED ==

A2 - Regular Access
Target: Scientific or innovation teams with proven prior experience
Platforms: Deucalion, MareNostrum 5, Cirrus+ Stratus
Duration: 12 months maximum
Resource Limits: CPU 100,000 to 3,000,000 core.hours; GPU up to 10,000 hours
One submission per institution per typology

A3 - Large-Scale Access
Target: Teams with consolidated experience requiring substantial computational resources
Platforms: Deucalion or MareNostrum 5 (not Cirrus+)
Duration: 12 months maximum
Resource Limits: CPU 3,000 to 30,000,000 core.hours; GPU up to 100,000 hours
One submission per institution per typology

== VIA RÁPIDA (FAST TRACK) ==

The Via Rápida route is exclusively for projects with prior scientific evaluation within the preceding 3 years. This fast-track process involves only technical and administrative validation - scientific merit re-evaluation is waived for qualifying projects. This allows experienced teams to access larger-scale computational resources more quickly without repeating full scientific review cycles.

== COMPUTATIONAL MODELS AVAILABLE ==

1. HPC - High Performance Computing
   Deucalion (Portuguese national petascale supercomputer), MareNostrum 5 (Barcelona), parallel processing, GPU nodes, high-memory configurations

2. IA - Artificial Intelligence Computing
   AI research and data science workloads, machine learning, deep learning, large model training

3. SCC - Scientific Cloud Computing
   Cirrus+ Stratus platforms, virtual machines, containers, databases, web services, federated cloud access, virtual research environments

== OBJECTIVES ==

The Advanced Computing Projects competitions aim to increase national and international competitiveness of Portuguese science and technology, fostering innovation, knowledge transfer, and alignment with the UN 2030 Sustainable Development Goals.

FCT aggregates national advanced computing resources, promoting cooperation among centers and developing national and international partnerships. The competition allocates computational resources from RNCA through competitive procedures.

== EVALUATION AND SELECTION ==

Applications in Lote E Via Rápida undergo technical and administrative validation only:
T1: Technical adequacy to advanced computing resources
T2: Reasonableness of requested resources
T3: Planning of resource utilization

Evaluation Classification: Accepted or Not Accepted (binary)
No full scientific merit review is conducted in Via Rápida - this is waived given prior evaluation history.

This competition is co-financed through Portugal's Recovery and Resilience Plan (PRR) and European Union NextGenerationEU funds, as well as regional co-financing programs including Norte 2030, Lisboa 2030, Algarve 2030, COMPETE 2030, and Portugal 2030.

== REGULATORY FRAMEWORK ==

The competition is regulated under Regulamento de Projetos de Computação Avançada nº 10/2022 (Diário da República). The regulation establishes eligibility, typologies, evaluation criteria, and resource allocation procedures across all lots and editions.

Managed by FCT (Fundação para a Ciência e a Tecnologia, I.P.) in coordination with FCCN (Fundação para a Computação Científica Nacional) which operates the RNCA infrastructure.

== AVAILABLE PLATFORMS ==

Deucalion - Portuguese national petascale supercomputer hosted at Minho Advanced Computing Center (MACC), University of Minho. Part of EuroHPC Joint Undertaking. Primary platform for A3 large-scale projects.
MareNostrum 5 - Barcelona Supercomputing Center (BSC), Spain. Available to Portuguese researchers under RNCA bilateral agreements.
Cirrus+ Stratus - Cloud platform for SCC access type (A2 only, not A3).

== HISTORICAL CONTEXT AND PROGRAM SCOPE ==

The competition has been running since its first edition with continuous expansion. The 6th edition spans multiple lots (A through E and beyond), with each lot opening sequentially to allow rolling access. Lote E is among the later lots in the sequence, incorporating the Via Rápida pathway as a permanent feature for A2 and A3 typologies.

Previous editions demonstrated high demand from Portuguese research institutions and companies seeking to leverage advanced computing infrastructure for competitive European research projects, industrial innovation, and collaboration with EuroHPC partners across EU member states.

The program supports Portugal's national strategy for advancing scientific computing capabilities, reducing dependency on commercial cloud providers, and building a national ecosystem of advanced computing expertise aligned with European research infrastructure policies.
