== TYPOLOGIES COVERED ==

A0 - Experimental Access
Target: Teams without prior RNCA (Rede Nacional de Computação Avançada) experience
Duration: 3 to 6 months
Resource Limits: Up to 50,000 CPU core.hours; up to 500 GPU.hours
Purpose: Testing and exploring HPC (High Performance Computing), IA (Artificial Intelligence Computing), Cloud, or visualization resources
One submission per institution per typology

A1 - Development Access
Target: Software performance testing, code optimization, and scalability projects
Duration: 3 to 12 months
Resource Limits: Up to 100,000 CPU core.hours; up to 5,000 GPU.hours
Activities: Performance benchmarking, scalability testing, code refactoring, optimization
One submission per institution per typology

== COMPUTATIONAL MODELS AVAILABLE ==

1. HPC - High Performance Computing
   Parallel processing applications, GPU access, high-memory machines, Deucalion supercomputer, MareNostrum 5, Cirrus+ Stratus

2. IA - Artificial Intelligence Computing
   Supporting research utilizing artificial intelligence and data analysis algorithms, machine learning, deep learning workloads

3. SCC - Scientific Cloud Computing
   Complex applications, web services, databases, virtual machines, containers, federated cloud access, virtual research environments

Company restrictions: Project must involve pre-commercial research/innovation; collective company applications cannot exceed 50% of total computational allocation.

== OBJECTIVES ==

The Advanced Computing Projects competitions aim to increase national and international competitiveness of Portuguese science and technology (aumentar a competitividade nacional e internacional da ciência e tecnologia), fostering innovation, knowledge transfer, and alignment with the UN 2030 Sustainable Development Goals.

FCT aggregates national advanced computing resources, promoting cooperation among various centers and developing national and international partnerships. The competition allocates computational resources from RNCA through competitive procedures.

== EVALUATION AND SELECTION ==

Applications undergo simplified technical validation only (no final numerical scoring in Lote E):
T1: Technical adequacy to advanced computing resources
T2: Reasonableness of requested resources
T3: Planning for resource utilization

Evaluation Classification: Accepted or Not Accepted (binary decision)
Processing: Accepted applications handled by batch and submission order until resource quotas exhaust.

Lote E employs the "via rápida" (fast-track) route exclusively, involving technical and administrative validation only - limited to projects with prior scientific evaluation within the preceding 3 years.

No new submission batches open if previous lot resources are depleted.

== REGULATORY FRAMEWORK ==

The competition is regulated under Regulamento de Projetos de Computação Avançada nº 10/2022, published in Diário da República. The regulation establishes the general framework for all lots and typologies across all editions of the competition.

Co-financed under PRR (Plano de Recuperação e Resiliência), NextGenerationEU, and European Regional Development Fund (FEDER). The program is managed by FCT (Fundação para a Ciência e a Tecnologia, I.P.) in coordination with FCCN (Fundação para a Computação Científica Nacional).

== AVAILABLE PLATFORMS ==

Deucalion - Portuguese national petascale supercomputer, hosted at Minho Advanced Computing Center (MACC), University of Minho. Part of EuroHPC Joint Undertaking.
MareNostrum 5 - Barcelona Supercomputing Center (BSC), Spain. Available to Portuguese researchers under RNCA agreements.
Cirrus+ Stratus - Cloud and virtual research environment platforms for SCC access type.

== HISTORICAL CONTEXT ==

The competition has been running since its first edition. The 6th edition expands across multiple lots (A through E and beyond), opening access to a broader range of institutions and company types than previous editions. The Via Rápida (fast-track) component of Lote E is specifically designed to expedite access for teams with demonstrated prior track records.
