Description:
Regulation (EU) 2025/327 (EHDS - European Health Data Space) reinforces citizens' rights to access and control their personal electronic health data. This action builds capacity across the EU health sector to implement EHDS requirements.

The action is divided into three work strands:

Work Strand 1 - Primary Use (Citizens' Rights):
Build capacity of public authorities and healthcare providers to deploy services and systems supporting citizens' rights under Chapter II of EHDS. Provide guidance for deployment of digital health services supporting citizen rights. Develop a community of practice for health data primary use.

Work Strand 2 - Secondary Use (Data Reuse):
Build capacity of health data holders to improve data quality, create dataset descriptions and labels for EHDS secondary use. Develop a toolbox for data holders to support dataset description and data quality labeling.

Work Strand 3 - Service Provider Training:
Train service providers, particularly SMEs, in EHDS compliance and implementation. Develop a training framework for service providers and conduct training sessions.

Cascading Funding Mechanism:
The project manages a portfolio of individual grants through a cascading funding mechanism. Maximum 120,000 EUR per third party supported through this mechanism.

Expected outcomes:
- Guidance documents for digital health service deployment
- Community of practice for health data primary and secondary use
- Large-scale deployment and capacity building across EU member states
- Toolbox for dataset description and data quality labeling
- Dataset descriptions and labels for EHDS secondary use
- Training framework for service providers
- Training sessions delivered to SMEs and healthcare service providers
- Business model for sustainable EHDS implementation
- Trained service providers across the EU

Regulatory Framework:
- Regulation (EU) 2025/327 - European Health Data Space (EHDS)
- EEHRxF (European Electronic Health Record Exchange Format)
- Health Data Access Bodies established under EHDS
