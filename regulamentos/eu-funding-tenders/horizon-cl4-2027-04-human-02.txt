## Complete Description

Expected Outcome:

The co-programmed European Partnership for Virtual Worlds aims to cultivate a thriving industrial and end-user ecosystem across the EU, encompassing the entire virtual worlds value chain. It will actively engage with industrial and societal sectors to deliver comprehensive resources including funding, expertise, and technology access.

Project results should contribute to: reviewing and updating the Strategic Research and Innovation Agenda for Virtual Worlds ensuring usefulness, openness, interoperability, inclusivity, sustainability and trustworthiness aligned with EU values; strengthening the European Virtual Worlds Partnership through continuous support; reinforcing competitive ecosystem with European companies leading in Virtual Worlds adoption and deployment; reinforcing connections among Horizon Europe initiatives, Digital Europe Programme, and other EU, national and regional programmes; implementing widespread awareness and outreach initiatives; increasing adoption of virtual worlds that are open, accessible, inclusive, interdisciplinary, safe, ethical and respecting European legal frameworks including privacy and security across all Member States and Associated Countries; strengthening and promoting standardisation for virtual worlds technologies supporting the EU regulatory framework.

Scope:

This Coordination and Support Action advances the community by reinforcing the Virtual Worlds Partnership and its Strategic Research and Innovation Agenda. It establishes a strong, inclusive network uniting academia, industry, public actors and end-users, including major European industrial sectors and stakeholders, ensuring coordination at regional, national and European levels.

The action strongly promotes Virtual Worlds technologies adoption across all Member States and Associated Countries, emphasizing geographical distribution and value chain breadth.

Outreach programmes will enhance understanding, awareness and acceptance of Virtual Worlds solutions, building trustworthiness.

The action supports standardisation efforts promoting interoperable, open, trustworthy and ethical Virtual Worlds solutions by mobilising stakeholders and organising European representation in standardisation working groups supporting Commission regulatory frameworks.

Proposals must involve and be driven by relevant field representatives including academia, Research and Technology Organisations, and industry encompassing SMEs.

The Commission considers proposals with approximately 36-month durations appropriate, though other durations remain eligible.

Proposals should incorporate effective Social Sciences and Humanities discipline contributions and expertise to enhance societal research impact.

Key stakeholders should prepare, manage and coordinate this CSA directly supporting the Virtual Worlds Partnership.

Beneficiaries transferring ownership or granting exclusive licenses must formally notify the granting authority before transfer, which may object within four years post-action completion.

This topic implements the co-programmed European Partnership on Virtual Worlds, requiring proposals allocating tasks to cohesion activities with Partnership-funded related actions including CSA HORIZON-CL4-2025-03-HUMAN-17.
