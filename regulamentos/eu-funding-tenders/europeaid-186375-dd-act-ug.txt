Title: Civil Society Organisations in-country call for proposals - Uganda 2026
Reference: EuropeAid/186375/DD/ACT/UG (also referenced as DD/ACT/Multi in some EEAS publications)
Programme: NDICI Global Europe - Thematic Programme for Civil Society Organisations 2021-2027 (CSO Thematic Programme)
Source: EU Delegation Uganda + EEAS Uganda page
Original call URL: https://www.eeas.europa.eu/delegations/uganda/call-proposals-%E2%82%AC51-million-civil-society-organisations-country-call-proposals_en

OVERVIEW
The EU Delegation to Uganda has launched a Civil Society Organisations in-country call for proposals with a total envelope of EUR 5.1 million. The call promotes sustainable, inclusive and rights-based management of the environment and natural resources, with focus on forests, land use and related value chains, and supports a more inclusive and equitable local economy in areas linked to EU Global Gateway investments in renewable energy.

GLOBAL OBJECTIVE
Strengthen the role of Ugandan civil society organisations in:
1. Promoting sustainable, inclusive and rights-based management of the environment and natural resources, with particular focus on forests, land use and related value chains.
2. Contributing to a more inclusive and equitable local economy in thematic areas and geographical locations where the EU and Team Europe are engaged in Global Gateway investments in renewable energy, including through enhanced access to affordable, reliable and clean energy, decent work and diversified livelihoods.

KEY DEADLINES AND CALENDAR
- Information session: 6 May 2026 at 10:00 AM (online).
- Concept note submission deadline: 4 June 2026.
- Full application deadline: only invited concept-note shortlistees, after evaluation.
- Two-stage process: mandatory concept note, then full application by invitation.

INDICATIVE BUDGET
Total envelope: EUR 5,100,000.

LOTS / FUNDING STREAMS
The call includes two interconnected priorities (treated as guiding objectives, not separate lots):
- Priority A: Environment and natural resources management (forests, land use, value chains).
- Priority B: Inclusive local economy linked to renewable energy Global Gateway investments.

Applicants may propose actions addressing one or both priorities in an integrated manner.

ELIGIBILITY (LEAD APPLICANTS)
- Civil Society Organisations (CSOs): NGOs, foundations, networks, professional and producer associations.
- Established in: EU 27 Member States, Uganda, NDICI Global Europe eligible countries.
- Cannot apply as a natural person.
- Co-applicants: at least one Ugandan co-applicant strongly recommended.

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES
- Sustainable forest management and forest landscape restoration.
- Community forestry and customary land tenure support.
- Sustainable agriculture and agroforestry value chains.
- Combating illegal logging, deforestation and land grabbing.
- Indigenous and local community land rights.
- Renewable energy access for rural and underserved communities (mini-grids, solar, biomass).
- Decent green jobs and skills for the energy transition.
- Diversified livelihoods around Global Gateway energy investment areas.
- Inclusive and gender-sensitive participation in environmental and energy governance.
- Watchdog and monitoring activities on environmental compliance.

ELIGIBLE COSTS
Standard NDICI Global Europe categories: staff, travel, equipment and supplies, local office, services, sub-granting (typically up to 30% of EU contribution), audit, visibility/communication, indirect costs (flat rate up to 7%).

CO-FINANCING
EU contribution typically up to 95% of eligible costs.

PROJECT DURATION
Typically 36-48 months for CSO thematic in-country calls of this scale.

APPLICATION PROCESS
- Online submission via PROSPECT platform of the EU Funding & Tenders Portal mandatory.
- Two-stage: mandatory concept note, then full application for invited shortlist.
- Languages: English.

EVALUATION CRITERIA
Concept notes: relevance to environment, natural resources and inclusive local economy in Uganda (35-40 points), design and methodology (20-25), feasibility and partnership (20), cost-effectiveness (15-20).

NOTES
The call is aligned with the EU Global Gateway strategy in Uganda (renewable energy investments, transport corridors, digital infrastructure) and complements the EU bilateral programme. The connection between civil society action and large infrastructure investments is a distinctive feature of the 2026 Uganda CSO call, designed to ensure that EU investments deliver inclusive, sustainable benefits at community level.
