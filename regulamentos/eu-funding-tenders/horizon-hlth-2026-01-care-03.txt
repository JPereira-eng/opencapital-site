## Description Summary

This topic addresses overuse, misuse and underuse of healthcare services through evidence-based research. The initiative aims to enable healthcare providers and policymakers to identify inefficiencies, equip professionals with implementation tools, and help patients understand quality improvements.

### Key Objectives

1. Understanding & Measurement: Develop methodologies to identify and measure low-value care throughout healthcare processes, testing related indicators for improved efficiency.

2. Analysis & Insights: Identify overuse, misuse, underuse, and unwarranted variation across different care stages, providing actionable recommendations for stakeholders.

3. Strategy Development: Create and pilot innovative reduction strategies demonstrating scalability across diverse European health systems.

### Eligible Activities
- Clinical studies providing evidence on intervention value
- Digital and AI-based analysis tools for identification
- Healthcare payment system evaluation
- Registry collaboration across regions/countries
- International comparisons and best practice transfers
- Gender-sensitive approaches addressing inequalities

## Expected Outcomes

Healthcare providers should employ evidence-based indicators and methodologies to identify low-value care practices and opportunities for improvement. Professionals need knowledge to implement guidelines reducing ineffective activities while maintaining quality. Patients benefit from more effective, higher-quality healthcare. Systems achieve enhanced safety, efficiency, and environmental sustainability through resource reallocation.
