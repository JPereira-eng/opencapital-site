## Full Description

### Expected Outcomes
The initiative targets three primary outcomes:

1. Strengthened capacity of life sciences actors in science communication, risk communication, public outreach, and citizen engagement.
2. Enhanced public awareness of risks and benefits through showcasing R&I developments and their societal impact.
3. Development of innovative approaches to engage the public in R&I activities in the life sciences, with an emphasis on inclusive and participatory approaches.

### Scope
The topic addresses critical gaps in public understanding and trust regarding life sciences innovations. Current challenges include rapid disinformation spread and insufficient public engagement mechanisms. The initiative emphasizes that citizen engagement is particularly critical in areas like agriculture and food technology.

### Required Activities
Proposals must address all four key activities:

1. Advisory Support & Training: Bilateral engagement with life science stakeholders to upskill them in science communication and risk communication. Selected projects should establish links with the European Competence Centre for Science Communication (launching 2027).

2. Citizen Engagement Guidance: Produce and disseminate guidance on engaging citizens upstream in the development, co-production, and co-design of life sciences innovation, with focus on deliberative participation and ethical considerations.

3. Risk Communication Repository: Create an accessible repository of tools for life science stakeholders on risk communication, gathering existing resources (from EU-funded projects and WHO guidelines) and establishing long-term sustainability strategies beyond project lifecycle.

4. Community Engagement Activities: Design and implement community engagement activities in the life sciences, in partnership with relevant local actors, such as science museums, R&I organisations, and/or community organisations. At minimum, one activity must focus on agriculture and food technology.
