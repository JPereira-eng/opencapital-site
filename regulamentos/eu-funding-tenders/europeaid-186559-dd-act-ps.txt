Programme: NDICI Global Europe - Thematic Programme for Civil Society Organisations (CSO) and Thematic Programme for Human Rights and Democracy (HRD)
Source: EU Representative Office West Bank and Gaza Strip + EEAS Palestine pages

OVERVIEW
The European Union Representative Office to the West Bank and Gaza Strip has launched a joint call for proposals under the Thematic Instruments for Civil Society and Human Rights and Democracy. The call mobilises approximately EUR 5.5 million in grants to support Palestinian civil society organisations to protect human rights, reinforce democratic governance, and expand civic space.

GLOBAL OBJECTIVE
Contribute to the upholding of democratic principles, the promotion and protection of human rights, and the strengthening of CSOs as independent actors of good governance and development in Palestine.

KEY DEADLINES AND CALENDAR
- Concept note submission deadline: extended to align with the joint NDICI thematic cycle, deadline confirmed in the EU Funding & Tenders Portal under reference 186559.
- Information sessions: organised in person and virtually, dates published on the EU Representative Office Palestine website.
- Two-stage process: concept notes first, full applications from shortlisted invited.

INDICATIVE BUDGET
Total envelope: approximately EUR 5,500,000.
Split between two thematic streams (lots):

LOTS / FUNDING STREAMS
- Lot 1 - Thematic Programme for Civil Society Organisations (CSO):
  Goal: strengthen Palestinian CSOs as independent actors of good governance, advocates, monitors and facilitators of dialogue between government, communities and the private sector.
  Indicative envelope: portion of the EUR 5.5 million.

- Lot 2 - Thematic Programme for Human Rights and Democracy (HRD):
  Goal: support implementation of the EU Action Plan on Human Rights and Democracy 2025-2027 in the Palestine context. Protect and promote universal human rights and fundamental freedoms (defenders, freedom of expression, equality, transitional justice).

ELIGIBILITY (LEAD APPLICANTS)
- Non-profit legal entities: civil society organisations, NGOs, foundations, networks, platforms, national human rights institutions.
- Established in: EU 27 Member States, Palestine, NDICI Global Europe eligible countries.
- Cannot apply as a natural person.
- At least one Palestinian co-applicant strongly encouraged for both lots.
- International organisations: may participate as co-applicants but typically not as lead under thematic CSO programmes.

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES
- Strengthening CSOs as independent governance actors and watchdogs.
- Defending human rights defenders and at-risk groups (women, journalists, minorities).
- Supporting freedom of expression, association and peaceful assembly.
- Promoting equality and combating discrimination.
- Supporting transitional justice, accountability and rule of law.
- Civic education, monitoring, advocacy and policy engagement.
- Direct support to defenders at risk and emergency response mechanisms (HRD lot).

ELIGIBLE COSTS
Standard NDICI Global Europe categories: staff, travel, equipment and supplies, local office, services, sub-granting where allowed, audit, visibility/communication, indirect costs (flat rate up to 7%).

CO-FINANCING
EU contribution typically up to 95% of eligible costs.

PROJECT DURATION
Typically 24-36 months under NDICI Palestine thematic envelope.

APPLICATION PROCESS
- Online submission via PROSPECT platform of the EU Funding & Tenders Portal mandatory.
- Two-stage: mandatory concept note, then full application for invited shortlist.
- Languages: English (Arabic context annexes accepted).

EVALUATION
Concept notes: relevance (40 points), design (20), effectiveness/feasibility (20), sustainability (10), cost-effectiveness (10). Full applications: methodology, partnership composition, capacity, budget realism.

CONTACT
EU Representative Office to the West Bank and Gaza Strip, Jerusalem. Reference EuropeAid/186559/DD/ACT/PS in all correspondence. Specific call mailbox published on the Delegation website.

DOCUMENTS
Full Guidelines for Applicants, annexes (concept note template, full application template, budget, logical framework, declarations) available via EU Funding & Tenders Portal under reference 186559.

NOTES
This is the 2025-2026 cycle of the joint NDICI thematic call for Palestine, complementing the 2022-2023 envelope (EuropeAid/175xxx/DD/ACT/PS) and aligned with the EU Action Plan on Human Rights and Democracy 2025-2027. The joint format (CSO + HRD in one call) was introduced to streamline the application burden on Palestinian CSOs operating in a contested operating environment.
