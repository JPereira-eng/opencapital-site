## Description

Expected Impact:

Projects shall deliver results addressing expected outcomes with dedicated KPIs including: tangible capacity increases in EU-based production of targeted energetic components demonstrating "competitiveness"; improved resilience and geographical distribution of supply reducing single points of failure; strengthened availability and security of supply for priority defence production lines; and measurable lead-time reductions with reserved capacity and trained workforce.

Proposals shall define KPIs such as added throughput, time-to-rate, lead-time reduction, yield improvements, qualified output, reserved capacity parameters, trained staff numbers, and risk mitigation indicators including raw-material securing, safety performance, supply-chain robustness and obsolescence management.

Physical and cyber protection work packages shall deliver measurable security improvements evidenced through threat assessments supporting security measures and KPIs including protected areas coverage, detection performance, response readiness, and critical vulnerabilities mitigated. These KPIs link to overall project risk registers demonstrating reduced risk exposure and improved production continuity.

Objective:

This call reinforces EU industrial production capacity of defence products including components and raw materials. The topic strengthens competitiveness, responsiveness and capacity of the European Defence Technological and Industrial Base to reinforce production capacity for energetic components constituting critical bottlenecks for key defence products across land, air, naval and underwater domains: rockets, aerial bombs, mines and loitering munition; missiles; tank, artillery and counter-unmanned systems ammunition; small-calibre cartridges for dismounted soldiers.

Actions shall contribute to competitiveness by securing specific production capacity for: propellant powder (nitrocellulose, single, double and triple-base powder, modular charge); explosives (nitroglycerine, TNT, RDX, HMX, PETN, NTO, primers/fuses); propulsion systems; warheads and electronic fuze; filling plants; and access to relevant raw materials.

As industrial reinforcement actions, proposals shall integrate robust approaches managing key risks including safety, supply-chain and obsolescence, life-cycle and maintainability, and industrialisation. Proposals shall account for necessity to upgrade quickly targeted defence products based on evolving battlefield lessons.

Applicants may include optional dedicated work packages implementing supporting actions to reinforce the physical and cyber protection of the production capacity targeted by the action, including security against UxS/drone-enabled threats. The objective reduces disruption, sabotage or interference affecting production continuity, ramp-up and deliveries, complementing overall risk-management approaches and supporting achievement of industrial KPIs for lead time and capacity.

Scope:

Proposals shall implement Industrial Reinforcement Actions targeting specific defence products and production capacities listed under objectives pursued. Proposals demonstrate criticality of targeted defence products and associated production capacity, justifying urgency of reinforcing availability and security of supply through proposed industrial reinforcement activities.

Proposals set out industrial reinforcement plans supported by risk-management approaches addressing identified risks and measures ensuring availability and security of supply, including risks specific to energetic materials such as process safety, hazardous materials handling, permits, and upstream precursor availability.

Activities linked to physical and cyber protection work packages may include expert threat assessment, procurement, installation and initial integration of proportionate solutions and efforts connecting to national or European security systems for counter-UxS site protection measures (detection/alerting and authorised protective measures) and cyber protection measures of production-relevant networks and systems (monitoring, hardening and secure access). This includes obtaining EU Cybersecurity Certification schemes for products used in industrial reinforcement activity activities, acceptance testing and operational setup for effective deployment, working towards NIS2 Directive compliance and alignment with standards including ISO/IEC 27001 and IEC 62443.

Physical and cyber protection work packages shall clearly specify protected assets, threat assessments for protected sites supporting security measures, interfaces with production continuity and KPIs, explain how measures reduce risks impacting core industrial results including lead-time reduction, throughput/capacity increase, reserved capacity and workforce readiness, and present efforts linking respective site security systems with national and/or European security systems.

Actions shall not have started before March 5, 2024 and shall not be completed before Grant Agreement signature. Duration and starting date set in Grant Agreement. Action shall have indicative duration of three to five years; completed no later than December 31, 2033.

Maximum consortium size: 15 legal entities.
