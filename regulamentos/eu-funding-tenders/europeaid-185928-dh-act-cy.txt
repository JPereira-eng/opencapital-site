EuropeAid/185928/DH/ACT/CY - Cypriot Civil Society in Action X (CSIA X)

Title: Cypriot Civil Society in Action (tenth edition - CSIA X)
Reference: EuropeAid/185928/DH/ACT/CY
Programme: Aid Programme for the Turkish Cypriot community
Source: European Commission Representation in Cyprus + civicspace.eu
Original call URL: https://cyprus.representation.ec.europa.eu/news/call-proposals-worth-over-eu2-million-launched-support-civil-society-organisations-throughout-cyprus-2026-04-20_en

OVERVIEW
The European Commission has launched the tenth edition of the Cypriot Civil Society in Action grant programme (CSIA X), worth EUR 2,250,000. The call supports civil society organisations (CSOs) across Cyprus with a particular focus on strengthening the Turkish Cypriot community. CSIA is a long-standing initiative to foster a dynamic, inclusive and sustainable civil society reflecting and promoting EU values and standards in Cyprus.

GLOBAL OBJECTIVE
Foster a dynamic, inclusive and sustainable civil society in Cyprus, particularly within the Turkish Cypriot community, that reflects and promotes EU values, supports socio-economic change, and contributes to bi-communal confidence-building and dialogue.

KEY DEADLINES AND CALENDAR
- Concept note submission deadline: 16 June 2026 at 15:00 Brussels time (16:00 Cyprus time).
- Information sessions: April-May 2026, online and on site.
- Full application deadline: only invited concept-note shortlistees, after evaluation.
- Restricted format: applicants submit concept notes initially, with full applications requested by invitation.

INDICATIVE BUDGET
Total envelope: EUR 2,250,000.

LOTS / FUNDING STREAMS (3 STRANDS)
- Strand 1 - Strengthening civil society participation in socio-economic change and decision-making.
  Focus: CSO contribution to public policy, advocacy on social and economic issues, civic engagement.

- Strand 2 - Bi-communal confidence-building actions promoting dialogue.
  Focus: cross-community dialogue, joint projects between Greek Cypriot and Turkish Cypriot civil society, reconciliation initiatives.

- Strand 3 - Projects by first-time applicants or organisations new to EU funding.
  Focus: capacity development of grassroots and emerging Turkish Cypriot CSOs, lower-volume grants with simplified procedures.

GLOBAL OBJECTIVE BY STRAND
The three strands together address the dual goal of strengthening Turkish Cypriot civil society capacity and supporting bi-communal cooperation toward an eventual settlement.

ELIGIBILITY (LEAD APPLICANTS)
- Civil society organisations registered or operating in Cyprus.
- Particular focus on Turkish Cypriot community organisations.
- Greek Cypriot CSOs eligible particularly under Strand 2 (bi-communal).
- First-time applicants or organisations new to EU funding eligible under Strand 3.
- Non-profit nature required.

CO-APPLICANTS
- Bi-communal partnerships strongly encouraged for Strand 2.
- International partnerships permitted but local Cyprus partnership required.

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES
- Civic engagement and advocacy on socio-economic issues.
- Capacity building of Turkish Cypriot CSOs.
- Cross-community dialogue platforms and joint actions.
- Civic education on EU values, democracy and the rule of law.
- Cultural and educational exchanges between communities.
- Support to underrepresented groups (women, youth, minorities) in civic life.
- Public awareness campaigns on common challenges (environment, social issues, EU integration).
- Strengthening journalism, freedom of expression and pluralistic media.

ELIGIBLE COSTS
Standard EU grant categories: staff, travel, equipment and supplies, local office, services, sub-granting where allowed, audit, visibility/communication, indirect costs (flat rate up to 7%).

CO-FINANCING
EU contribution typically up to 95% of eligible costs given the protective nature of the Aid Programme for the Turkish Cypriot community.

PROJECT DURATION
Typically 12-24 months for Cypriot Civil Society in Action grants, with Strand 3 projects often shorter (12-18 months).

APPLICATION PROCESS
- Restricted call: concept notes first, full applications by invitation.
- Online submission via PROSPECT platform of the EU Funding & Tenders Portal mandatory.
- Languages: English, Turkish (annexes), Greek (annexes).

EVALUATION CRITERIA
Concept notes: relevance to strand objectives (35-40 points), design and methodology (20-25), feasibility and partnership (20), cost-effectiveness (15-20).

NOTES
CSIA is funded under the Aid Programme for the Turkish Cypriot community (Council Regulation (EC) No 389/2006), which is the principal EU instrument supporting reconciliation, economic integration and EU-related preparations of the Turkish Cypriot community pending a comprehensive settlement of the Cyprus issue. CSIA X is the latest in a recurring series of grants funded under this programme since the early 2000s.
