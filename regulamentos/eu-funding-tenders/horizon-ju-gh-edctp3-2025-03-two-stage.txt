NAME: Global collaborative action for strengthening the Regional Networks of Excellence and Epidemic Preparedness Consortia
PROGRAMME: Horizon Europe - Global Health EDCTP3 Joint Undertaking
SHARD: eu-horizon
DETECTED: 2026-04-26

DESCRIPTION:
This call (HORIZON-JU-GH-EDCTP3-2025-03-two-stage) covers one topic: HORIZON-JU-GH-EDCTP3-2025-03-NETWORKS-01-two-stage, targeting global collaborative action for strengthening the Regional Networks of Excellence and Epidemic Preparedness Consortia.

The Global Health EDCTP3 Joint Undertaking supports clinical research and development to address poverty-related diseases in sub-Saharan Africa, aiming to accelerate the development of new or improved health technologies, improve research capacity, and develop a sustainable clinical research infrastructure.

TOPICS:
- HORIZON-JU-GH-EDCTP3-2025-03-NETWORKS-01-two-stage: Global collaborative action for strengthening the Regional Networks of Excellence and Epidemic Preparedness Consortia

BUDGET:
Total indicative budget: approximately EUR 53 million for this call.
20 applications were submitted requesting approximately EUR 261.5 million in total.

ADDITIONAL AWARD CRITERIA:
This call includes additional award criteria considering meaningful and significant effects enhancing impact, as well as leveraging of financial and/or in-kind contributions from contributing partners that are equal or greater than the requested JU contribution.

ELIGIBILITY:
Legal entities based in an EU Member State or an Associated Country can serve as project coordinators.
For legal entities from non-EU countries not associated to Horizon Europe: a consortium can only assign a project coordinator role to an institution from a country with a bilateral science and technology (S&T) agreement with the EU.
South Africa is the only EDCTP Association member from sub-Saharan Africa with such an S&T agreement with the EU.
Eligible organisations include universities, research organisations, public health institutes, and clinical research networks in sub-Saharan Africa and Europe.

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES:
- Strengthening regional networks of excellence for epidemic preparedness
- Building and sustaining epidemic preparedness consortia across sub-Saharan Africa
- Collaborative research and capacity building between African and European institutions
- Training and mentoring researchers in participating countries
- Data sharing and joint governance frameworks

SELECTION CRITERIA:
- Scientific and technological excellence
- Quality and efficiency of implementation
- Quality and relevance of the impact
- Meaningful participation from sub-Saharan African partners
- Financial leverage and co-investment from third parties

TWO-STAGE PROCESS:
Stage 1: Short outline proposal (expression of interest)
Stage 2: Full proposal invitation for selected outlines
