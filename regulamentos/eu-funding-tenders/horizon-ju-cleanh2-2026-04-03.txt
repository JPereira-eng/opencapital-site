DESCRIPTION
The call addresses hydrogen and ammonia as enablers for decarbonizing power generation. Ammonia presents advantages as a carbon-free energy carrier with superior storage and transport properties compared to hydrogen. However, direct gas turbine combustion poses challenges: narrow flammability limits causing flame instability, fuel-bound nitrogen creating unacceptable NOx and N2O emissions, and toxic compound emissions (ammonia slip, HCN).

The initiative seeks breakthrough combustion technology enabling clean, efficient operation with ammonia or ammonia-hydrogen blends under gas turbine conditions (high temperature and pressure). Expected outcomes include fundamental combustion physics understanding, reliable fuel-flexible systems, and full-scale demonstrators by 2035.

KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS (KPIs)
Proposals must address:
- Dual-fuel operation spanning 100% ammonia-based fuels to 100% backup fuels
- NOx emissions below 300 mg/MJ for ammonia fuels
- Maximum efficiency reduction below 2 percentage points
- N2O, NH3, HCN emissions below 10 ppm (@15% O2)

EXPECTED OUTCOMES
- Fundamental understanding and detailed characterization of combustion physics of ammonia-based fuels at gas turbine-relevant operating conditions
- Reliable and fuel-flexible combustion system verified for clean, efficient operation with ammonia-based fuels or hydrogen at high temperature/pressure
- Breakthrough multi-fuel combustion system technology and full-scale gas turbine demonstrator by 2035
- Substantiated technological feasibility assessment including safety, environmental, social and economic benefit/risk evaluation

SCOPE AND RESEARCH AREAS
The proposal must investigate:
- Chemical kinetics validation for ammonia oxidation against flame properties measurements
- Fundamental combustion properties at minimum 10 bar pressure
- Static/dynamic flame stabilization control (flashback, blow-out, thermo-acoustic instabilities)
- Novel injection schemes and staging strategies
- Demonstration in full-scale combustor hardware (TRL 5)
- Advanced combustion modeling
- Safety and risk assessment for ammonia utilization in power generation

TECHNOLOGY READINESS LEVEL
TRL 5 expected by project end
