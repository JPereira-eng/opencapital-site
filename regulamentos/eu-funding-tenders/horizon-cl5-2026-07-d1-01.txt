HORIZON-CL5-2026-07-D1-01
Next Generation Climate Monitoring and Related Capabilities

CALL IDENTIFIER: HORIZON-CL5-2026-07
TOPIC: HORIZON-CL5-2026-07-D1-01
STATUS: Open
PROGRAM: Horizon Europe - Climate Science and Solutions (HORIZON.2.5.1)
ACTION TYPE: RIA (Research and Innovation Actions)
FUNDING MODEL: Lump sum grants

DEADLINE: 21 April 2026, 17:00 CET (postponed due to technical issues)
OPENING DATE: 17 September 2025

BUDGET:
- This topic (D1-01): EUR 15,000,000
- Expected grants: 3
- Grant range: EUR 5,000,000 per project
- Total call (all D1 topics): EUR 82,000,000

OBJECTIVES:
Projects should advance climate data records, exploit recently rescued data streams, and develop methods to improve water-energy-carbon cycle consistency. Expected outcomes include improved climate data, strengthened EU climate sovereignty, and climate information distillation for policy making.

THREE PRIORITY AREAS:
A - Next generation climate data records
B - Earth system reanalyses
C - Climate forcing and emission datasets

DESPESAS ELEGIVEIS / ELEGIBILIDADE:
- EU member states and associated countries (Annex B)
- Lump sum grants - budget justification required for cost categories C/D if exceeding 15% of personnel costs
- Must use Copernicus and/or Galileo/EGNOS for satellite data
- Must address only ONE priority area
- Must promote FAIR principles and CMIP alignment
- Must assess US divestment impacts on climate monitoring
- Must coordinate with IPCC, WCRP, GCOS, and Copernicus initiatives

CRITERIOS DE SELECAO:
Awards prioritize ranking order while ensuring balanced portfolio coverage with at least one proposal funded within each priority area (highest ranked within each).

KEYWORDS: Climate monitoring, Essential Climate Variables, data records, reanalysis, climate forcing, emissions datasets
