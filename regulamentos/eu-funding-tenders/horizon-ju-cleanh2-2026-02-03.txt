New thermal insulation concepts for bulk liquid hydrogen shipping

KEYWORDS
Hydrogen; bulk; containment; liquid hydrogen; shipping; thermal insulation

ELIGIBLE COUNTRIES
EU Member States and Associated Countries

DESCRIPTION
This topic addresses the development of validated containment concepts for bulk liquid hydrogen (LH2) shipping. The initiative focuses on improving insulation robustness through exploration of advanced technologies including multi-layer insulation (MLI), variable-density multilayer insulation (VDMLI), spray-on foam insulation (SOFI), aerogels, and nano-cellular foams. Current vacuum-jacketed dewar tanks have limitations in large-scale production and vulnerability to single-point failures. The project requires comprehensive investigation of thermal management, structural integrity, manufacturing processes, safety performance, and scalability to support European competitiveness in LH2 trade markets.

EXPECTED OUTCOMES
- Economically viable technology strengthening European LH2 import/export capabilities
- Safe, cost- and energy-efficient tanks scalable to 250,000 m3 per ship, compliant with IMO standards
- Foundation for large-scale trade supporting shipping, heavy-duty mobility, aviation, and industrial applications
- Promotion of European industry leadership in LH2 insulation technologies

SCOPE
Development requires assessment of regulatory requirements, insulation system design, 2D numerical analysis of heat ingress, structural support optimization, prototype manufacturing (minimum 30 m3 capacity), scalability analysis, techno-economic viability demonstration, manufacturing process development, digital twin creation, FMEA analysis, safety performance validation, fire resistance testing, and pre-normative standardization cooperation with industry stakeholders.

TECHNOLOGY READINESS LEVEL
TRL 3 (start) to TRL 5 (project completion)
