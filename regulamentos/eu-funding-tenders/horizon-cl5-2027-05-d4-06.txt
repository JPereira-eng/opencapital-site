## DESCRIPTION

This funding opportunity addresses the growing energy demands of the Information and Communications Technology sector while maintaining alignment with climate neutrality and sustainability objectives. Small IT rooms and edge data centres play an essential role in the dual green and digital transition, making it critical to evaluate energy and resource consumption across the entire ICT value chain.

Recent analysis indicates that consumer device energy consumption represented approximately 50% of total ICT technology energy use in 2020, with device production accounting for roughly 20% and data centre operations representing about 15%. This distribution is anticipated to shift significantly by 2030, as overall ICT technology energy consumption is projected to increase by 50% during this decade.

The European Commission has established a strategic objective requiring data centres to achieve climate neutrality, energy efficiency, and resource efficiency by 2030. However, most current efforts concentrate on data centres falling under the Energy Efficiency Directive recast, leaving smaller facilities with unmet needs.

**Project Scope Requirements:**

Proposals must address the following elements comprehensively:

First, demonstrate advanced, cost-effective approaches to thermal management and excess heat recovery, potentially incorporating heat storage and upgrading capabilities, with subsequent reuse in local or nearby locations. Focus should target existing IT rooms in buildings or small edge data centres within the 100-500kW range not covered by the Energy Efficiency Directive recast. Solutions must account for thermal envelope and building characteristics through simulation or digital twin technologies. Integration with building automation and control systems is essential, with potential for enhanced self-assessment and self-optimization features at multiple operational levels. Projects should incorporate compatibility with existing Building Management Systems to facilitate development of energy services. Demonstration sites must be located in both cold and warm climate Member States or Associated Countries to address climate-specific thermal management challenges.

Second, develop viable business cases for state-of-the-art excess heat recovery and reuse solutions. These may include recovering excess heat for existing building systems such as heating or domestic water production, connecting to local district heating and cooling networks, or redirecting heat to nearby sites that can derive meaningful benefit from the recovered thermal energy.

**Expected Outcomes:**

Projects are anticipated to generate improved open access to performance data regarding IT infrastructure operations, facilitate sharing of best practices among facility owners and operators, and distribute valuable knowledge across the IT and building sectors. Additionally, new IT room designs should emerge that optimize waste heat valorization through innovative thermal management techniques, processes, and digital tools.

**Technology Readiness Level:** Activities are expected to reach TRL 6-7 by project completion, though work may commence at any TRL stage.

**Synergies:** Projects are encouraged to establish connections with funded initiatives from Cluster 4, specifically HORIZON-CL4-2027-04-DATA-09 addressing energy efficiency and sustainability of AI data processing in data centres demonstration pilots.
