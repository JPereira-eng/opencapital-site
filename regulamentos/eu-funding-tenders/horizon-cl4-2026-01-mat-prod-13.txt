Monitoring of secondary raw materials (CSA)

Budget Overview (2026):
- Total: 4.000.000 EUR
- Expected grants: 1

Description:
This Coordination and Support Action focuses on strengthening EU autonomy in raw materials supply through secondary raw materials monitoring. The action covers the full pipeline from in-situ sampling across EU regions to harmonized UNFC format reporting, focusing on critical waste streams.

Key Expected Outcomes:
- Enhanced knowledge base on secondary raw materials in EU and third countries, covering potential, resource estimation, production, and biodiversity impact
- Accelerated commercial development of secondary raw materials projects
- Market trend analysis reports and supply-demand bottleneck identification
- Improved EU strategic planning capacity for raw materials

Scope:
- Data collection via in-situ sampling across EU regions
- Harmonized UNFC format reporting
- Focus on critical waste streams: waste batteries, WEEE, mining waste, slags and ashes, and construction and demolition waste
- Biodiversity footprint assessment and natural capital accounting
- Analysis of anthropogenic resources (urban mining potential)

Elegibilidade:
- Participants limited to Member States, associated countries, OECD countries, African Union members, MERCOSUR, CARIFORUM, Andean Community, and countries with EU strategic raw materials partnerships
- Entities controlled by ineligible countries excluded
- Financial and operational capacity assessment required

Conditions:
- Admissibility per Annexes A and E
- Eligible countries per Annex B
- Evaluation per Annex D
- Submission system opened January 6, 2026
- Budget justification required for cost categories C/D exceeding 15% of personnel costs (updated Feb 16, 2026)

Keywords: Secondary raw materials, circular economy, biodiversity footprint, natural capital accounting, waste streams, critical raw materials, anthropogenic resources
