EuropeAid/185831/DD/ACT/SY - Syria: Justice, Freedom of Expression and Social Cohesion

Title: Support to justice, freedom of expression and social cohesion in Syria
Reference: EuropeAid/185831/DD/ACT/SY
Programme: NDICI Global Europe - Thematic Programme on Human Rights and Democracy / Civil Society Organisations (Syria response framework)
Source: fundsforNGOs aggregator and EEAS announcements
URL of original call page: https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/prospect/external/publishedcalls.htm?callId=185831

OVERVIEW
This EU call for proposals supports Syria's transition following the fall of the Assad regime in December 2024. The action funds civil society organisations and public sector operators working on access to justice, transitional justice, media freedom and journalist protection, and social cohesion in post-conflict Syria. The total envelope of EUR 13.35 million reflects the EU's reinforced commitment to supporting an inclusive Syrian-led and Syrian-owned transition.

BUDGET AND LOTS
Total budget: EUR 13.35 million, distributed across four lots:
- Lot 1 - Access to Justice: EUR 2,000,000.
  Focus: legal aid, justice system monitoring, victim support, access to courts for vulnerable groups.
- Lot 2 - Transitional Justice: EUR 2,000,000.
  Focus: documentation of human rights violations, support to victims, restorative and accountability mechanisms, missing persons, reparations.
- Lot 3 - Media Freedom and Journalist Protection: EUR 675,000.
  Focus: safety of journalists, independent media, pluralism, protection of media workers and digital rights.
- Lot 4 - Social Cohesion: EUR 4,000,000.
  Focus: dialogue facilitation, mediation, community-level cooperation, inclusive recovery and peacebuilding initiatives.

The remaining envelope (approximately EUR 4.675 million from EUR 13.35M) covers complementary lots on civil society capacity, women and youth participation, or is reserved for reserve list and contingency. Full lot allocation must be confirmed on the PROSPECT call page.

ELIGIBILITY
Eligible applicants:
- Non-profit organisations (NGOs, civil society organisations, foundations).
- Public sector operators (universities, research centres, public bodies operating without profit).
- Local authorities.

Geographic eligibility:
- Established in the European Union or in selected neighbouring countries.
- Specific attention to organisations with operational capacity in Syria or working on the Syria response from neighbouring countries (Lebanon, Jordan, Türkiye, Iraq, Egypt).

PARTNER REQUIREMENTS
- Collaborative implementation in Syria is expected.
- Partnerships with Syrian local stakeholders, diaspora organisations and grassroots civil society are strongly encouraged.
- Co-applicants from Syria can participate where their legal status allows.
- Sub-granting to local Syrian organisations is foreseen as a key delivery modality, subject to call rules.

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES
Lots 1 and 2 (Justice and Transitional Justice):
- Legal aid delivery to vulnerable populations (women survivors of gender-based violence, detainees, displaced persons, minorities).
- Justice system monitoring and trial observation.
- Institutional strengthening of justice actors and bar associations.
- Victim support, psychosocial care and restorative justice mechanisms.
- Documentation of violations, evidence preservation, missing persons identification.

Lot 3 (Media Freedom):
- Safeguarding journalists through safety training, equipment, emergency assistance and legal support.
- Fostering independent media outlets, including local and exile media.
- Promoting media pluralism, fact-checking and counter-disinformation initiatives.
- Digital rights, online safety and protection from surveillance.

Lot 4 (Social Cohesion):
- Dialogue facilitation among communities, ethnic and religious groups.
- Mediation services and conflict transformation at community level.
- Community-level cooperation projects and inclusive recovery.
- Reintegration of returnees and displaced persons.
- Cultural and intergenerational initiatives that rebuild trust.

ELIGIBLE COSTS
Standard NDICI cost categories apply:
- Personnel.
- Travel and missions, including in Syria where security allows.
- Equipment and supplies.
- Sub-grants to local Syrian organisations (key modality).
- Local office and operational costs.
- Audit, monitoring, evaluation, communication and visibility.
- Indirect costs at standard flat rate (up to 7 per cent).

DEADLINE
18 May 2026 (Brussels time).

EVALUATION CRITERIA
Proposals are assessed on:
- Relevance to chosen Lot objectives and to the Syria transition context.
- Impact potential and ability to reach vulnerable populations.
- Feasibility under Syria's security and operational constraints.
- Sustainability of the action and ownership by Syrian local stakeholders.
- Quality of partnership with local actors and sub-granting strategy.
- Operational and financial capacity of the consortium.
- Risk management, do-no-harm and conflict-sensitivity approaches.

PROGRAMME CONTEXT
- Reflects the EU's reinforced engagement with Syria following December 2024 political transition.
- Aligned with the EU Action Plan on Human Rights and Democracy 2025-2027.
- Complements humanitarian assistance through ECHO and stabilisation programmes under the EU's Instrument contributing to Stability and Peace.
- Part of the EU's broader Mediterranean Pact and Syria response framework.
