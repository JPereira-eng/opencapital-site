# HORIZON-CL2-2026-01-DEMOCRACY-09 Topic Details

**Title:** Citizenship education as part of lifelong learning

**Call Identifier:** HORIZON-CL2-2026-01

**Topic Identifier:** HORIZON-CL2-2026-01-DEMOCRACY-09

---

## Description

Expected Outcome:

Projects should contribute to ensuring that policymakers, educators, and relevant actors have tools and recommendations to expand lifelong civic and citizenship learning opportunities supporting democracy and democratic participation, including through digital means and civic tech. Additionally, policymakers at local, regional, national and EU levels need tools and recommendations for enabling citizen participation in democratic life across all backgrounds, particularly for those facing participation barriers.

Scope:

Healthy democracies require citizens capable of making informed choices in free elections. Meaningful, inclusive citizen participation strengthens representative democracies and builds institutional trust. Education fundamentally supports active democratic participation and civic engagement across lifelong learning processes for all age groups regardless of educational or socioeconomic background.

Continuous learning about democratic processes, civic rights and obligations remains crucial for all ages. Technological, geopolitical and economic changes challenge social cohesion and equal democratic participation. This topic examines how citizenship education within lifelong learning boosts democratic involvement and civic engagement.

Research should investigate vocational education and training (VET) at secondary and post-secondary levels, including apprenticeships, or adult education programmes in raising civic knowledge, political awareness, and attachment to democratic values including human dignity, freedom, equality and rule of law. Projects examine either VET or adult education programmes and investigate links between participation and increased civic actions like voting, candidacy, public policy engagement, volunteering or civil society involvement.

Research may explore how VET or adult programmes support citizens including persons with disabilities in enhancing digital and media literacy skills, understanding the democratic information ecosystem, recognizing disinformation and using digital tools for democratic engagement.

Research should test and pilot innovative citizenship awareness methods and curricula for VET or adult education teachers and students. A gender and intersectional perspective, along with perspectives of marginalized and underrepresented groups facing accessibility barriers or disengagement risks, should be systematically integrated.

Research may examine digital and media literacy fostering critical thinking, shaping citizen media competencies, and developing best practices. Proposals may address democratic participation among aging populations facing digital barriers and propose participation solutions. While digital tools support engagement, research may examine non-digital person-to-person approaches supporting participation, including inter-generational settings.

Proposals should seek collaboration with the European Partnership on Social Transformations and Resilience.

Citizenship is recognized as the ability to act responsibly and participate fully in civic life, grounded in an understanding of social, economic, legal and political structures. This involves understanding and evaluation of civic and democratic concepts, institutions and processes, including democracy, media literacy, crisis preparedness and respect for others and freedom of speech.
