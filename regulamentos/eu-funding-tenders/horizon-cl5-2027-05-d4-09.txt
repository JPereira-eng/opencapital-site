## Complete Description

The project scope focuses on demonstrating innovative systems to recover industrial excess and waste heat, converting it to useful mechanical or electrical power. This addresses a critical gap in industrial energy efficiency where significant thermal losses remain underutilized across European manufacturing sectors.

**Expected Outcomes:**

Projects must achieve measurable results across multiple dimensions. First, at least one industrial sector with substantial heating demands within the EU and Associated Countries should develop concrete pathways and viable business models that demonstrably enhance industrial excess and waste heat recovery coupled with power conversion. Second, projects should deliver measurable improvements in industrial excess and waste heat recovery and conversion operations, potentially integrated with energy storage systems. These improvements span multiple performance areas: enhanced energy efficiency, flexibility in electricity generation, climate and environmental protection, system reliability and safety, physical and cyber security robustness, and improved profitability metrics.

**Project Scope and Requirements:**

Better utilization of process excess and waste heat represents a substantial energy conservation opportunity for industrial operations. Converting recovered excess heat back into electricity enhances overall energy efficiency while mitigating increased electricity demand stemming from industrial electrification efforts. When combined with thermal or electrical storage, converted heat can provide flexible electricity generation to stabilize grid operations.

Successful projects must comprehensively address all the following components:

Development and validation of novel excess and waste heat recovery and conversion systems represents the foundational technical work. Projects must physically demonstrate, within one or more operational industrial sites, the practical recovery of industrial excess and waste heat, optionally combined with energy storage, and its subsequent conversion to mechanical or electrical power output. This demonstration phase is critical for validation.

Maximizing replicability and deployment across EU and Associated Countries industrial facilities within the same sector requires strategic planning. Before commencing design phases, comprehensive surveys and analysis of needs across most EU and Associated Countries factories in the targeted industrial sector(s) must be conducted to ensure the solution accommodates the broadest possible range of facility requirements.

Projects should identify and optimize common system components and establish standardized versions applicable across multiple installations. Developers must create comprehensive, openly accessible technical and economic guidelines, coupled with practical tools for integrating such systems into existing SCADA (Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition) systems and EMS (Energy Management System) infrastructures. Additionally, innovative business models for heat valorization should be developed, positioning flexible electricity generation as a grid-stabilization service.

Results dissemination, particularly targeting communities within cluster 4 'Digital, Industry and Space' private-public partnerships, ensures broader impact and uptake.

**Technology Readiness Level:** Activities are expected to achieve TRL 7-8 by project conclusion, though activities may commence at any TRL level.
