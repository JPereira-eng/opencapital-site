Description:

Expected Impact:
The financial assistance aims to develop and implement Projects of Common Interest (PCIs) and Projects of Mutual Interest (PMIs) toward achieving broader TEN-E and CEF energy policy objectives including further integration of an efficient and competitive internal energy market, interoperability of networks across borders and sectors, and facilitating decarbonization while ensuring supply security. This call aligns with the European Green Deal, Paris Agreement, 2030 climate and energy targets, and EU decarbonization objectives.

Expected Outcome:
This topic enables PCIs and PMIs implementation within trans-European energy networks. The call supports infrastructure projects with significant socio-economic benefits and greater solidarity among Member States that lack adequate market financing. Supported projects pursue European Green Deal goals, Paris Agreement commitments, and long-term decarbonization objectives, maximizing added value toward energy sector decarbonization.

Objective:
Supporting and contributing to PCI and PMI implementation.

Scope:
This topic refers to study projects preparing PCI or PMI implementation, including preparatory, mapping, feasibility, evaluation, testing and validation studies (including software), technical support measures, site/route identification, and financial package development. Only projects contributing to PCIs and PMIs identified in the Second Union list qualify for EU grant support. Eligible categories fall under TEN-E Regulation Article 24 and Annex II provisions.

Conditions:
Admissibility conditions, eligible countries, other eligibility conditions, financial and operational capacity requirements, exclusion criteria, evaluation processes, award criteria, scoring thresholds, and evaluation timelines are detailed in the call document sections 5-10.
