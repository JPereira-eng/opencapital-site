Ensuring comprehensive geographical coverage of the Network of Safer Internet Centres (SICs)

Description:
The objective contributes to the protection and empowerment of children online through comprehensive geographical coverage of national Safer Internet Centres (SICs) in the EU. The action seeks to enhance coverage by engaging SICs not previously funded through the Digital Europe Programme. Only one SIC per country is co-funded.

Required services for each SIC:
1. Awareness centre - for children, parents, and teachers about online opportunities and risks, including specific and emerging risks (harmful content, cyberbullying, age-inappropriate content, sexual extortion, addictive design, disinformation, AI-generated harmful content)
2. Helpline - for advice and support on children's technology use and mental health issues related to online activity
3. Hotline - for reporting Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM) online
4. Youth panel - for direct engagement with young people on online safety issues

Additional activities:
- Strengthen support to vulnerable children, including those with disabilities and from disadvantaged backgrounds
- Support monitoring of digital transformation impact on children
- Support implementation of EU strategies and legislation (Digital Services Act, UN General Comment No. 25)
- Promote training modules and expand BIK (Better Internet for Kids) Youth Ambassadors
- Provide trustworthy resources and campaigns
- Provide digital literacy training for children, parents, and educators
- Support parents and educators in managing children's online activity
- Identify emerging risks related to new technologies
- Cooperate with online platforms as trusted flaggers of illegal content

Target groups:
- Children (including those with special needs and from disadvantaged backgrounds)
- Parents
- Teachers and professionals working with children
- Youth
