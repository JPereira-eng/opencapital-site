EuropeAid/185516/DD/ACT/Multi - Marine Protected Areas Management (Gabon and regional)

Title: Support for the Management of Marine Protected Areas in Gabon
Reference: EuropeAid/185516/DD/ACT/Multi
Programme: NDICI Global Europe / EU External Action - PROSPECT (RELEX-PROSPECT)
Source: fundsforNGOs aggregator and EU funding portal listing

OVERVIEW
This EU action grant supports the regional governance and effective management of Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) in the Gulf of Guinea, with a focus on Gabon and complementary work in São Tomé and Príncipe and Cameroon. The action contributes to the preservation of marine biodiversity, the development of a sustainable Blue Economy and the strengthening of regional cooperation for ocean governance. Gabon already protects 26 per cent of its Exclusive Economic Zone through a network of 20 MPAs, and this action consolidates and replicates that model regionally.

BUDGET
- Total programme budget: EUR 2,000,000 (indicative).
- EU contribution: EUR 2,000,000.
- The action is structured as a single grant (multi-country); applicants may propose consortia with regional reach.

ELIGIBILITY
Eligible applicants:
- Experienced non-profit organisations capable of leading large-scale regional initiatives in marine conservation.
- Demonstrated capacity in managing marine and coastal projects at regional or sub-regional scale.
- Established in EU Member States, NDICI-Global Europe eligible countries, or in the target countries (Gabon, São Tomé and Príncipe, Cameroon).
- Civil society organisations, foundations, research institutions and qualified public bodies typically eligible under EU external action rules.

Geographic eligibility:
- Lead applicants and co-applicants must be established in countries eligible under NDICI-Global Europe.
- The action must be implemented in Gabon, São Tomé and Príncipe and Cameroon (multi-country dimension).

PARTNER REQUIREMENTS
- Co-applicant participation is encouraged.
- Partnerships with civil society organisations, local communities and the private sector are expected.
- Specific emphasis on inclusion of women, girls and youth in project governance and activities.
- Coordination with national MPA authorities and existing regional bodies (e.g. RAMPCO - the Network of Marine Protected Areas in Central Africa) is required.

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES
Five priority areas:
1. Regional governance strengthening and inter-state coordination among the three target countries on MPA management.
2. Development of a Blue Economy Facility supporting sustainable financing models for MPA management (carbon credits, biodiversity offsets, eco-tourism, sustainable fisheries).
3. Update of regional marine protected area strategies in coordination with the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN).
4. Direct support to the management of marine protected areas in São Tomé and Príncipe and Cameroon, including infrastructure, equipment, monitoring and surveillance.
5. Fisheries sustainability initiatives and supply chain improvements (artisanal fisheries, traceability, market access).

CROSS-CUTTING THEMES
- Gender equality and women's economic empowerment in coastal communities.
- Youth engagement and skills development for blue jobs.
- Climate adaptation and ocean climate resilience.
- Indigenous peoples and local communities' rights, including free, prior and informed consent (FPIC).

ELIGIBLE COSTS
Standard NDICI / EU external action eligible cost categories apply:
- Personnel (international and local staff).
- Travel and missions within the region.
- Equipment and supplies (boats, surveillance gear, scientific equipment) subject to depreciation rules.
- Local office and operational costs.
- Sub-grants to local CSOs and community-based organisations where allowed.
- Audit, evaluation, communication and visibility.
- Indirect costs at standard flat rate (up to 7 per cent).

DEADLINE
30 April 2026 (per fundsforNGOs aggregator; applicants must confirm on the PROSPECT call page).

EVALUATION CRITERIA
Proposals are assessed on:
- Experience in marine conservation and regional cooperation.
- Feasibility of the proposed work plan and partnerships.
- Expected impact on biodiversity, governance and local livelihoods.
- Alignment with programme priorities (regional approach, gender, youth, Blue Economy).
- Sustainability of results beyond EU funding.
- Financial and operational capacity of the consortium.

PROGRAMME CONTEXT
- Part of the EU's Team Europe Initiative on Sustainable Blue Economy in the Gulf of Guinea.
- Aligned with the EU Strategy for the Gulf of Guinea and the Africa-EU partnership on environment, climate and biodiversity.
- Contributes to the Global Biodiversity Framework target of protecting 30 per cent of marine and coastal areas by 2030 (the "30 by 30" target).
