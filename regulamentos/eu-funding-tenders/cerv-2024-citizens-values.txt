CERV-2024-CITIZENS-VALUES
Call for proposals for civil society organisations active on protecting and promoting Union values: call to intermediaries (giving financial support to third parties)

Identifier: CERV-2024-CITIZENS-VALUES
Call Identifier: CERV-2024-CITIZENS-VALUES
Framework Programme: Citizens, Equality, Rights and Values Programme (CERV)
Programme Period: 2021-2027
Status: Closed
Deadline Date: 7 March 2024, 17:00 (Brussels Time)
Budget Overview in EUR: 74,500,000 (2024)
Action Type: CERV-PJG CERV Project Grants (CERV Action Grant Budget-Based)

Description:
The initiative seeks to safeguard and advance fundamental rights and EU values through supporting civil society groups and strengthening their capabilities. It targets intermediary organisations across EU Member States that can enhance capacities of numerous local, regional, and national CSOs within CERV programme scope. Proposals must incorporate financial support provisions for third-party CSOs alongside capacity-building activities. Priority focus includes small, remote, and rural organisations with constrained resources. Only activities advancing rule of law, fundamental rights, and democratic principles receive support.

This is the EU Commission's flagship instrument to channel structural support to the civil society ecosystem in Member States, including in countries where civic space is shrinking. The intermediary model is deliberate: a relatively small number of strong CSOs receive funding from the Commission and act as re-granting bodies that distribute targeted micro-grants and capacity building to a much larger number of grassroots organisations.

Scope: Eligible activities include capacity building, organisational development, advocacy training, networking, exchange of best practices, support to coalition-building, and the operation of re-granting schemes targeting small CSOs working on rule of law, anti-discrimination, fundamental rights, civic engagement, and democratic participation. Geographic priority is given to areas where access to EU funding is structurally limited.

Eligibility:
Applicants must be non-profit civil society organisations, networks of CSOs, or non-profit foundations established in eligible countries (EU Member States and CERV-associated countries). Consortium structure is required. Demonstrated track record in re-granting and CSO capacity-building is essential.

Despesas elegiveis: pessoal, subcontratacao, viagens, eventos, comunicacao, custos de gestao do esquema de re-granting, financial support to third parties (FSTP), formacao, networking, indirect costs flat rate.

Criterios de selecao: relevancia para os valores UE e prioridades CERV, abrangencia geografica e tematica, qualidade do esquema de re-granting, alcance esperado em CSOs pequenos e remotos, capacidade do consortium, sustentabilidade.

Conditions: Eligible countries, eligibility and admissibility requirements, evaluation criteria, and timeline are detailed in the call document. Evaluation period: March-July 2024. Grant agreement signature: September-December 2024.

Keywords: Civil society, Civil society organisations, Democracy, Democratic engagement and civic participation, Rule of law, Valorisation and capacity building, European Values, awareness raising, capacity building, fundamental rights, human rights defender, re-granting.
