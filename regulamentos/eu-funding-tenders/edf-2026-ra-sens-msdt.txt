Description:

The high speed and significant manoeuvrability of hypersonic vehicles, particularly hypersonic glide vehicles, create difficult conditions for target detection and tracking, which are exacerbated by changes in radar cross section characteristics. Radar waveforms and signal processing become essential to counter the effects induced by hypersonic threat dynamics. In addition, the last decade has seen a global increase in the use of unmanned aerial vehicles for battlefield intelligence gathering and reconnaissance. Anti-UAV radars therefore need to be developed to detect, identify and track such systems. Innovative approaches including AI and cognitive methods can provide means to select optimal waveform receiver filter combinations to maximise radar detection and tracking performance using feedback loops between received and transmitted signal characteristics.

This topic focuses on developing an engineering development model capable of demonstrating maturity of innovative radar technologies and supporting feasibility assessment regarding time, investment cost, complexity and military applicability. The ultimate aim is reducing risks associated with future full sensor development incorporating these features.

Beyond defence technologies, this topic contributes to STEP objectives targeting deep and digital technologies.

Targeted activities: Generating and integrating knowledge, studies and design.
