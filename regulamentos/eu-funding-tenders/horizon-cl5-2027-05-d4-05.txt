## Description & Scope

The call seeks innovative approaches to accelerate deep renovation of buildings by modernizing the construction sector through robotics and automation. Applicants must demonstrate innovative on-site robotic and automated techniques for building renovation and the construction of new buildings with at least one technique involving 3D printing technology.

**Key Requirements:**

Proposals must develop techniques that are specifically for building-site applications with flexibility across various building types and compatibility with existing market materials. Solutions focusing on prefabrication in off-site workshops fall outside scope parameters. Human-robot collaboration and worker safety must receive appropriate consideration throughout development.

Demonstrations must occur in at least three demonstrations to investigate their applicability for a variety of building typologies and climatic conditions. Projects must address energy renovation of existing buildings, though new construction projects are also eligible. Demonstration sites should represent a relevant part of the European building stock.

**Expected Outcomes:**

Projects should deliver improved resource efficiency compared to current practices and improved accuracy (designed vs. as-built) of on-site renovation and construction works, compared to current best practices. Proposals targeting significant reduction in time spent by building professionals on site and reduced GHG emissions at the construction site align with call priorities.

The European Commission's Joint Research Centre (JRC) may provide scientific advice and technical analysis of solutions and technologies, support to the transfer of research output to standards, and access to the European Laboratory for Structural Assessment research infrastructure for full-scale testing of buildings.

**Technology Readiness Level:** Activities must achieve TRL 6-8 by the end of the project with flexibility regarding starting points.
