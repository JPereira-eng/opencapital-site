EuropeAid/186514/DD/FPA/EG - Strengthening Civil Society in Egypt (FPA Restricted Call)

Title: Strengthening Civil Society in Egypt
Reference: EuropeAid/186514/DD/FPA/EG
Programme: NDICI Global Europe - Civil Society Organisations Thematic Programme
Type: Restricted call - Framework Partnership Agreement (FPA) holders only
Source: EU Delegation Egypt + DevelopmentAid grant 1640657
Original call URL: https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/prospect/external/publishedcalls.htm?callId=186514

OVERVIEW
The EU Delegation to Egypt has launched a restricted call for proposals limited to organisations holding a Strategic Partnership for Strengthening Civil Society in Egypt Framework Partnership Agreement (FPA) with the European Commission. The call mobilises EUR 5,000,000 to support Egyptian civil society organisations as actors of governance, development and rights protection.

KEY DEADLINES AND CALENDAR
- Status as of late April 2026: Open. Concept note submission window typically 6-8 weeks from publication.
- Information session: organised by EU Delegation Egypt for FPA partners, dates published via direct invitation to FPA holders.
- Full deadline details available only to FPA partners through restricted access on the EU Funding & Tenders Portal under reference 186514.

INDICATIVE BUDGET
Total envelope: EUR 5,000,000.
Grant range per project:
- Minimum grant: EUR 600,000
- Maximum grant: EUR 1,000,000
Estimated 5-8 grants funded under this call.

ELIGIBILITY (LEAD APPLICANTS)
- RESTRICTED: limited to European Commission Framework Partners holding a "Strategic Partnership for Strengthening Civil Society in Egypt" Framework Partnership Agreement.
- Applicants must be non-profit legal entities established in Egypt or in EU 27 / EEA countries (Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway).
- Applicants must have direct responsibility for project preparation and implementation, not act as intermediary.
- Co-applicants permitted: must also be non-profit and from eligible countries (Egypt, EU 27, EEA).

ELIGIBLE COUNTRIES OF ESTABLISHMENT
EU 27 Member States, Egypt, Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway.

ELIGIBLE SECTORS / THEMATIC PRIORITIES
- Community Development & NGOs
- Culture & Arts
- Environment & Climate
- Gender & Human Rights
- Education and capacity building
- Civic engagement and freedom of expression

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES (NDICI CSO TEMPLATE)
- Strengthening Egyptian CSOs as independent governance actors.
- Capacity building of grassroots organisations and networks.
- Support to advocacy and policy engagement on social, environmental and rights issues.
- Promotion of inclusive participation of women, youth and marginalised groups.
- Cultural and educational projects supporting civic values and dialogue.
- Sub-granting to local CSOs allowed within standard NDICI rules.

ELIGIBLE COSTS
Standard NDICI Global Europe categories: staff, travel, equipment and supplies, local office, services, sub-grants, audit, visibility, indirect costs (flat rate up to 7%).

CO-FINANCING
EU contribution typically up to 95% of eligible costs. FPA holders may negotiate higher EU share within the strategic partnership framework.

PROJECT DURATION
Typically 36-48 months under multi-year NDICI Egypt CSO programmes.

APPLICATION PROCESS
- Restricted to FPA holders. Non-FPA organisations cannot apply directly.
- Online submission via PROSPECT platform / EU Funding & Tenders Portal.
- Two-stage: concept note (mandatory), then full application for shortlisted.
- All application templates and annexes accessed through partner portal account.

EVALUATION
Standard EU restricted-call grid: relevance to FPA strategic partnership priorities (35-40 points), design and feasibility (20-25), effectiveness and sustainability (20), cost-effectiveness (15-20).

CONTACT
EU Delegation to Egypt, Cairo. Direct contact for FPA partners through assigned programme officers and the FPA contract reference. Reference EuropeAid/186514/DD/FPA/EG in all correspondence.

DOCUMENTS
Full Guidelines for Applicants and annexes available only to FPA partners through EU Funding & Tenders Portal restricted access under reference 186514.

NOTES
The FPA modality concentrates EU support on a small number of trusted long-term partners with proven track record in Egypt. Non-FPA organisations interested in Egyptian civil society work must wait for the next open NDICI Egypt CSO call (typically biennial) or partner as co-applicant under an FPA holder-led project. The FPA framework runs in parallel to NDICI in-country open calls and to the global Civil Society Cooperation in the field of Youth FPA 2026-2027.
