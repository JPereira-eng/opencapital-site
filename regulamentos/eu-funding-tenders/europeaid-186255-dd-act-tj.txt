Reference: EuropeAid/186255/DD/ACT/TJ
Programme: NDICI Global Europe - Thematic Programme on Human Rights and Democracy (HRD)
Source: EU Delegation Tajikistan + EEAS Tajikistan page

OVERVIEW
The EU Delegation to Tajikistan has launched a call for proposals to strengthen human rights, fundamental freedoms and rule of law in Tajikistan. The call is funded under the NDICI Global Europe Thematic Programme on Human Rights and Democracy and supports civil society organisations (CSOs) acting as defenders of universal rights and democratic principles in the Central Asian context.

GLOBAL OBJECTIVE
Promote the protection and advancement of human rights, fundamental freedoms and rule of law in Tajikistan, supporting independent civil society organisations and human rights defenders.

KEY DEADLINES AND CALENDAR
- Information session: 5 May 2026 at 11:00 (Brussels date and time).
- Concept note submission deadline: 2 June 2026 at 14:00 (Brussels date and time).
- Full application deadline: only invited concept-note shortlistees, after evaluation.
- Two-stage process: mandatory concept note, then full application by invitation.

INDICATIVE BUDGET
Total envelope: not publicly specified, but consistent with NDICI HRD country allocations for Central Asia, indicative envelope likely in the EUR 1.5-3 million range across multiple grants.
Grant min/max: typical HRD thematic programme range EUR 250,000 - EUR 1,000,000 per project.

LOTS / FUNDING STREAMS
Single thematic envelope - Human Rights and Democracy. No formal lots, but applications expected across:
- Defense of human rights and freedoms
- Strengthening of rule of law institutions
- Civic space protection
- Women, youth and minority rights

ELIGIBILITY (LEAD APPLICANTS)
- Civil Society Organisations (CSOs): non-profit, non-governmental organisations.
- National Human Rights Institutions, foundations, networks/platforms, independent research centers, think tanks.
- Established in: EU 27 Member States, Tajikistan, NDICI Global Europe eligible countries.
- Cannot apply as a natural person.
- Co-applicants: at least one strongly recommended, partnerships with Tajik local actors valued.

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES
Actions aimed at promoting human rights, fundamental freedoms and rule of law:
- Defense of human rights defenders (HRDs) and at-risk groups.
- Support to freedom of expression, association and peaceful assembly.
- Promotion of equality and combating discrimination.
- Support to victims of torture and ill-treatment.
- Anti-discrimination of minorities, women, persons with disabilities, LGBTIQ+ persons.
- Civic education on rights and democratic participation.
- Strengthening rule of law institutions and accountability mechanisms.
- Monitoring, advocacy, policy engagement at national and international level.

ELIGIBLE COSTS
Standard NDICI Global Europe categories: staff, travel, equipment and supplies, local office, services, sub-granting where allowed (typically up to 30% of EU contribution), audit, visibility/communication, indirect costs (flat rate up to 7%).

CO-FINANCING
EU contribution typically up to 95% of eligible costs for HRD calls given the protective nature of human rights work.

PROJECT DURATION
Typically 24-36 months under HRD thematic programme.

APPLICATION PROCESS
- Online submission via PROSPECT platform of the EU Funding & Tenders Portal mandatory.
- Two-stage: mandatory concept note, then full application for invited shortlist.
- Languages: English (Russian or Tajik annexes accepted for local context).
- Physical consultation of guidelines: 74 Adhamov Street, Dushanbe (EU Delegation Tajikistan).

EVALUATION CRITERIA
Concept notes: relevance to human rights and democracy priorities in Tajikistan (35-40 points), design and methodology (20-25), feasibility and risk management (20), cost-effectiveness (15-20).

EU Delegation to Tajikistan, Dushanbe.
Address: 74 Adhamov Street, Dushanbe (full guidelines available physically).
Reference EuropeAid/186255/DD/ACT/TJ in all correspondence.

DOCUMENTS
Full Guidelines for Applicants, annexes available via EU Funding & Tenders Portal under reference 186255 and through EU Delegation Tajikistan.

NOTES
The HRD thematic programme is a stand-alone NDICI envelope distinct from country bilateral cooperation, designed to allow EU support to CSOs even where bilateral cooperation with the host government is constrained. Tajikistan calls under HRD operate in a constrained civic space environment, requiring strong risk management and security protocols for grantees and beneficiaries.
