EuropeAid/186065/DD/ACT/IL - Call for Proposals (NDICI Global Europe - Israel)

Title: EU NDICI Global Europe - Thematic Programme for Civil Society Organisations & Thematic Programme for Human Rights and Democracy - Israel 2026

Reference: EuropeAid/186065/DD/ACT/IL
Programme: Neighbourhood, Development and International Cooperation Instrument (NDICI) - Global Europe
Source: EEAS Delegation Israel announcement
URL of original call page: https://www.eeas.europa.eu/delegations/israel/new-call-proposals-within-framework-neighbourhood-development-and-international-cooperation_en?s=200

OVERVIEW
The EU Delegation to Israel has launched a call for proposals under the Neighbourhood, Development and International Cooperation Instrument (NDICI) - Global Europe framework. The call is split into two thematic streams (lots) and aims to contribute to upholding democratic principles, promoting and protecting human rights, and strengthening Israeli civil society organisations as independent governance actors.

LOTS / FUNDING STREAMS
- Lot 1: Thematic Programme for Civil Society Organisations (CSO).
  Goal: strengthen Israel's CSOs as independent actors of good governance and development; support their work as advocates, monitors, and facilitators of dialogue between government, communities and the private sector.
- Lot 2: Thematic Programme for Human Rights and Democracy (HRD).
  Goal: support implementation of the EU Action Plan on Human Rights and Democracy 2025-2027 in the Israel context; protect and promote universal human rights and fundamental freedoms.

GLOBAL OBJECTIVE
Contribute to the upholding of democratic principles, to the promotion and protection of human rights, and to the strengthening of Israel's civil society organisations as independent actors of good governance and development.

KEY DEADLINES AND CALENDAR
- Concept note submission deadline: Tuesday, 26 May 2026, 22:00 (Brussels date and time).
- Online information session (in English): Wednesday, 15 April 2026, 10:30 (Israel time), online.
- Registration deadline for the information session: 14 April 2026.

APPLICATION PROCESS
- Online submission via the PROSPECT platform is mandatory for the call.
- A two-stage process is used: concept note first, then full application for those invited.
- Information session registration: email DELEGATION-ISRAEL-FCA-CALL-FOR-PROPOSALS@eeas.europa.eu with subject line "Info session: Ref. EuropeAid/186065/DD/ACT/IL".

ELIGIBILITY (ESSENCIAL)
Based on the standard NDICI Global Europe Thematic Programme template applied to past Israel calls (EuropeAid/180844, EuropeAid/186065 family):
- Lead applicants: non-profit legal entities, civil society organisations, NGOs, foundations, networks/platforms.
- Established in EU 27 Member States, Israel, or other NDICI-Global Europe eligible countries.
- Cannot apply as a natural person.
- At least one co-applicant is normally required; partnerships with Israeli local actors are encouraged.
- International organisations may participate as co-applicants but typically not as lead applicants under thematic CSO programmes.

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES (TYPICAL UNDER THIS THEMATIC PROGRAMME)
- Strengthening CSOs as independent actors and watchdogs.
- Defending human rights defenders and at-risk groups.
- Supporting freedom of expression, association and peaceful assembly.
- Promoting equality and combating discrimination (gender, minorities, LGBTIQ+, persons with disabilities).
- Supporting transitional justice, accountability and the rule of law.
- Civic education, monitoring, advocacy and policy engagement.

ELIGIBLE COSTS
Standard NDICI Global Europe rules apply (typical cost categories): staff, travel, equipment and supplies, local office, costs of services, sub-granting where allowed, audit, visibility/communication, indirect costs (flat rate up to 7%).

DURATION OF ACTION
Typical NDICI thematic programmes for CSO/HRD: 24 to 48 months depending on lot.

EU CONTRIBUTION
The standard NDICI thematic programme template applies an EU co-financing rate of up to 90 to 95 per cent of total eligible costs, with a balance to be financed by the applicant or other donors. Specific minimum and maximum grant amounts and the total available envelope per lot are detailed in the Guidelines for grant applicants on the EU Funding and Tenders Portal.

EVALUATION CRITERIA
Standard EU concept note and full application scoring:
- Relevance to call objectives, country context and Lot priorities.
- Design and methodology, including monitoring and evaluation.
- Effectiveness and feasibility of the action.
- Sustainability and impact, including ownership by local stakeholders.
- Cost effectiveness and budget realism.
- Operational and financial capacity of the applicant and partners.

NOTES
- Israel-specific calls under EuropeAid have historically targeted CSOs working on human rights of Palestinian and Israeli citizens, social inclusion, gender equality, persons with disabilities and minority rights.
- Full detailed specifications (total envelope, grant ranges per lot, eligible nationalities for co-applicants, scoring grid) are available through the EU Funding and Tenders Portal call page (PROSPECT) and the official Guidelines for grant applicants document.
