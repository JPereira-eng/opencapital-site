**Title:** Space critical equipment for EU non-dependence

**Identifier:** HORIZON-CL4-2027-SPACE-03-84

**Call Identifier:** HORIZON-CL4-2027-03

**Framework Programme:** Horizon Europe (HORIZON)

**Main Pillar:** Global Challenges and European Industrial Competitiveness / Digital, Industry and Space

**Type of Action:** HORIZON-RIA (Research and Innovation Actions)

**Planned Opening Date:** February 5, 2027

**Submission Deadline:** June 17, 2027

**Deadline Model:** Single-stage

**Budget Overview (2027):** €490,000 total; 1 expected grant; minimum contribution €250,000; maximum €300,000

---

## Description

Projects should reinforce EU strategic independence by reducing reliance on non-EU sources for critical space equipment and enabling domestic supply chains. Key outcomes include: unrestricted access to advanced space materials; independent operational capacity through resilient supply chains; competitive products matching international performance standards; and new commercial opportunities.

The initiative addresses gaps where EU solutions cannot meet current performance requirements and external alternatives face export restrictions. Development must prioritize EU-based supply chains, with external procurement only when necessary and subject to authority approval, ensuring trustworthy sources unaffected by trade controls.

Equipment selection will derive from institutional program requirements, stakeholder input, and the EU Observatory of Critical Technologies. The list is scheduled for 2026 validation with EU delegations.

Activities should leverage cross-industry collaboration and complement national and European space agency efforts, with clear identification of complementary work.

**Technology Readiness Level Target:** TRL 6 by project completion (per ISO 16290:2013)

## Conditions

**Admissibility:** 80-page proposal limit per Annex A and E specifications

**Eligible Participants:** Legal entities established in EU Member States, Norway, and Iceland only. Entities controlled by non-eligible countries require documented security guarantees ensuring: operational autonomy, restricted sensitive information access, national security clearances, and IP ownership remaining within eligible jurisdictions.

**Satellite Data Requirement:** Projects using Earth observation or positioning services must utilize Copernicus and/or Galileo/EGNOS systems

**Evaluation:** Award decisions prioritize ranked applications while ensuring at least one highest-ranked proposal per development area receives funding, provided thresholds are met. Evaluation committees include EU institution representatives.

**Security:** Some activities may involve classified background or security-sensitive results (EUCI/SEN per General Annexes Section B)
