European partnership on sustainable food systems for people, planet and climate

Identifier: HORIZON-CL6-2023-FARM2FORK-01-9

Status: Closed
Framework Programme: Horizon Europe (HORIZON)
Programme Division: HORIZON.2 (Global Challenges and European Industrial Competitiveness), HORIZON.2.6 (Food, Bioeconomy Natural Resources, Agriculture and Environment)
Budget Overview (EUR): Total indicative budget for partnership duration 175,000,000. Topic 9 allocation 45,000,000. Budget split: 22,500,000 (2023), 22,500,000 (2024).
Deadline Date: April 12, 2023
Action Type: HORIZON COFUND (Horizon Europe Programme Cofund Actions)

Description:
The partnership addresses transforming European food systems toward sustainability. It coordinates aligned research and innovation efforts across local, national, and global contexts using integrated transdisciplinary approaches. The initiative pools resources from participating national/regional research programs through joint calls delivering transnational proposals.

Key objectives include developing Strategic Research and Innovation Agendas, establishing food systems knowledge hubs with central platforms and transformative labs, providing place-based solutions through living labs, enabling knowledge sharing and scaling through science-policy interfaces, and strengthening quadruple helix stakeholder engagement.

The partnership targets nutrition advancement, climate action, environmental protection, circular economy principles, innovation support, and community empowerment. Expected duration spans seven to ten years with Commission support anticipated throughout Horizon Europe.

Scope: The European Partnership on Sustainable Food Systems is structured to align national funding agencies and regional programmes with the EU Farm-to-Fork strategy, the Green Deal, the Mission Soil Health, and Food 2030. It is the principal platform for joint transnational calls on food system transformation throughout the Horizon Europe duration.

Despesas elegiveis e modelo de financiamento:
Funding operates at 30% of eligible costs (Cofund rate). Multi-actor approach mandatory. Partners must provide financial or in-kind contributions. Participants may grant financial support to third parties (maximum 10,000,000 per entity for entire Horizon Europe duration).

Eligible costs cover personnel, subcontracting, equipment, consumables, travel, dissemination, third-party financial support to research projects selected through joint calls, and the partnership coordination overhead. The Cofund mechanism means the Commission tops up the budget that national/regional funders bring.

Criterios de selecao: relevancia para Farm-to-Fork e Green Deal, qualidade da governance da partnership, ambicao no alinhamento das agencias nacionais, qualidade dos joint calls planeados, abordagem multi-actor, dimensao quadruple helix, sustentabilidade da partnership pos-2030.

Conditions: Admissibility, eligibility, financial capacity requirements detailed in Work Programme General Annexes A-G.

Keywords: Citizen engagement, co-benefits, food 2030, food processing, food systems, governance, living labs, multi-actor approach, nutrition, partnership, sustainability, Farm-to-Fork, Green Deal.
