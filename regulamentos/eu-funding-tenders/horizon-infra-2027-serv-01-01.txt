# HORIZON-INFRA-2027-SERV-01-01 Funding Topic

**Title:** Access to research infrastructures, their resources and services: large-scale pilots for more integrated scheme across (sub)domains

**Identifier:** HORIZON-INFRA-2027-SERV-01-01

**Call Identifier:** HORIZON-INFRA-2027-01

**Call Title:** Research Infrastructures 2027

**Framework Programme:** Horizon Europe (HORIZON)

**Main Pillar:** Excellent Science > Research infrastructures

**Planned Opening Date:** February 5, 2027

**Deadline Date:** May 20, 2027

**Deadline Model:** Single-stage

**Budget Overview (2027):** €105,000,000 (for this specific topic action); total call budget across all related actions: €379.5 million

## Description (Full Content)

This initiative targets testing access programme-like projects providing transnational access (on-site or remote) and/or virtual access to integrated, customized research infrastructure installations and services. The scope encompasses frontier and curiosity-driven to applied research, offered by a wide range of complementary and interdisciplinary research infrastructures with experience in transnational access.

**Expected Outcomes:** Projects should enable more efficient access to the best research infrastructures available to researchers and innovators to conduct basic and applied research and foster innovation, irrespective of location. Additional outcomes include establishing a single-entry point access portal, integrated or interoperable catalogues of services and converging access conditions and selection procedures, advancing a longer-term sustainable access programme harmonised across different ESFRI domains, enabling breakthrough and leading-edge research enabled by advanced research infrastructure services, training a new generation of researchers, and promoting interdisciplinarity and sharing of information, knowledge and technologies across scientific fields.

**Three Defined Areas:**
- Area 1: Physical Sciences and Engineering; Data, Computing and Digital Research Infrastructures
- Area 2: Health & Food; Social Sciences and Humanities
- Area 3: Environment; Energy

Proposals must explicitly state which area they address.

**Core Requirements:** Proposals should provide a wide, inclusive and comprehensive portfolio of complementary research infrastructure services of European interest, including data services. Minimum requirement: at least two ESFRI Landmark and/or European Research Infrastructure Consortium (ERIC) as beneficiary.

The initiative emphasizes access includes ad hoc users' training and scientific and technical support. Proposals should reserve sufficient resources for training opportunities targeting early-stage career researchers, or research infrastructure technicians from widening and candidate countries.

Data management, interoperability, and connections to the European Open Science Cloud should be addressed. Proposals should take due account of major European or international initiatives relevant in the domain and foster the use and deployment of (open) global standards.

**Outreach Requirements:** Proposals need an outreach and engagement plan to actively advertise their services to the research communities, notably from widening countries and candidate countries and to relevant industries, including SMEs. Support for developing research partnerships with researchers from widening and candidate countries is encouraged.

**Portal Development:** Access opportunities should be presented in a single-entry point portal for each pilot, building on the experience of past EU projects supporting access in the respective areas.

**Collaboration Imperative:** Proposals must ensure strong and continuous collaboration with related EU-funded initiatives, particularly cross-domain preparatory actions and other pilot projects. This collaboration should ensure a common front page to all above actions, set up by one of the pilots, providing at the minimum basic information such as highlighting the common objectives of EU supported access.

**Third-Party Access Provision:** Proposals may implement access through financial support to third parties (FSTP), with beneficiaries ensuring the conditions and requirements relating to access provisions are fulfilled. The mechanism allows a one stage selection procedure combining calls for researcher access and FSTP calls.

**Joint Research Centre Inclusion:** Proposals may consider including the Commission's Joint Research Centre, which operates an open access to JRC research infrastructures programme, providing since 2017 access and training and capacity building to a portfolio of 18 different facilities to more than 650 users from 250 institutions in Europe.

**Gender Dimension:** Integration is not mandatory but recommended where applicable in calls for access.

## Conditions

**Admissibility:**
- Proposals need not include exploitation and dissemination plans (main objective is service provision)
- Page limit: 100 pages
- Layout requirements detailed in Annex A and Annex E of Work Programme General Annexes

**Eligible Countries:** Described in Annex B of Work Programme General Annexes. Exceptionally, entities from Australia, Brazil, Canada, Chile, India, Japan, Mexico, New Zealand, Republic of Korea, Singapore, and USA providing state-of-the-art infrastructure access to EU researchers are eligible for funding.

**Special Conditions:**
- Joint Research Centre may participate as zero-funded beneficiary or associated partner
- Access provision activities must be included in proposals
- Applicants must read "Specific features for Research Infrastructures" section carefully

**Evaluation Criteria (Excellence Focus):**
- Extent to which access activities offer state-of-the-art infrastructures of European interest in the field, high quality services, and will enable users to conduct excellent research
- Degree of facilitation and integration of access procedures
- Service improvements and online service development

**Award Strategy:** To ensure balanced portfolio across areas, grants awarded not only by ranking but to highest-ranked proposals in each area meeting all thresholds.

**Financial Arrangements:**
- Eligible costs may use unit costs for transnational and virtual access (per Decision on unit costs for research infrastructures actions)
- FSTP maximum per third party: €300,000
- Beneficiaries grant royalty-free access to intellectual property relating to tools, standards, specifications, and outputs to EU institutions and other EU-funded projects
- Access mechanism to be defined in proposal, continuing during and beyond the lifespan of the Grant Agreement
