## Complete Description

This initiative seeks decentralized renewable energy systems addressing Africa's energy challenges. Local production and consumption, reduced transmission and distribution losses, and enhanced energy autonomy are core benefits.

Projects must demonstrate renewable energy deployment in African urbanized or rural contexts with socio-economic benefits including health improvements, job creation, and environmental protection using a Water-Energy-Food-Ecosystem approach.

Renewable Energy Valleys integrate multiple renewable energy technologies (solar PV, solar thermal, wind, mini-hydro, biomass, geothermal) with energy storage, grid infrastructure, and end-use applications across a defined geographical area. The valley concept enables aggregation of demand and supply, sector coupling between electricity, heat, cooling, transport and productive uses, and creation of energy communities with active participation of local stakeholders.

Key requirements include:
- Design, construction, commissioning and operation of demonstration installations in real African contexts
- Tailored value chain development leveraging local materials and workforce
- Technical and vocational training programs to build local skills for installation, operation, maintenance
- Life cycle analysis comparing solutions to fossil-based and grid-extension alternatives
- Circular economy adoption with mitigation of identified negative impacts on water, land, biodiversity and communities
- Gender-sensitive implementation benefiting vulnerable groups, women-led businesses, and youth

African SMEs are expected to play an important role in the overall value chain. EU private sector participation aligns with the Global Gateway Strategy priorities for sustainable infrastructure investment in Africa.

Projects must achieve TRL 8 by completion. Proven access to necessary land and/or permits for operation at the time of application is essential. Applicants must show valid land use rights, environmental clearance pathways, and engagement with national/regional authorities.

The action contributes to the African Union Agenda 2063 energy goals, the SE4ALL initiative, and the EU-Africa partnership on green energy. Projects should align with national energy plans of the participating African countries and seek synergies with multilateral development bank financing for scale-up beyond the demonstration phase.

Exploitation plans must include preliminary scalability and replication strategies, including business models that can sustain the renewable energy infrastructure beyond the project lifetime, with clear revenue mechanisms (tariffs, productive use anchor offtakers, results-based financing).

## Eligible Participants

Legal entities from African Union member states are eligible. Minimum requirement: at least two legal entities established in at least two different African Union member states must be part of the consortium.

---

## Key Conditions

- Single-stage submission process
- Expected 2 grants per topic (varying amounts: 4.5 million to 43 million EUR depending on sub-topic)
- Land/permit access evaluation is a major element of implementation quality assessment
- Satellite data use requires Copernicus and/or Galileo/EGNOS systems

Standard Horizon Europe conditions apply: Annex A (admissibility), Annex B (eligible countries), Annex C (financial capacity), Annex D (award criteria), Annex F (evaluation processes), Annex G (legal and financial set-up).

## Despesas Elegiveis e Criterios de Selecao
Despesas elegiveis: personnel costs, subcontracting, equipment, infrastructure, materials, training programs, indirect costs per Horizon Europe rules. Criterios de selecao per Annex D: excellence, impact, quality and efficiency of implementation, with special emphasis on land/permit access readiness, local SME involvement, and gender-sensitive implementation. TRL 8 by project end required.
