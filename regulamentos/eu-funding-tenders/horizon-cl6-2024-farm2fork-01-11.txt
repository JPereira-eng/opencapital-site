HORIZON-CL6-2024-FARM2FORK-01-11
EU-African Union, towards climate-neutral, social just fair trade food systems

Identifier: HORIZON-CL6-2024-FARM2FORK-01-11
Call Identifier: HORIZON-CL6-2024-FARM2FORK-01
Status: Closed
Framework Programme: Horizon Europe (HORIZON)
Programme Division: HORIZON.2 (Global Challenges and European Industrial Competitiveness), HORIZON.2.6 (Food, Bioeconomy Natural Resources, Agriculture and Environment)
Budget Overview in EUR: 9,000,000 (2024)
Deadline Date: 22 February 2024
Action Type: HORIZON-RIA (Research and Innovation Actions), Budget-Based Grant (HORIZON-AG), single-stage submission

Description:
This topic supports sustainable EU-African food trade systems addressing climate neutrality and fairness. It emphasises understanding trade barriers, certification schemes, and scaling climate-neutral supply models. The scope includes studying tipping points for scaling up climate-neutral food supply, analysing climate and biodiversity impacts from land-use changes, and clarifying non-tariff measures affecting intra-African and EU-AU trade relations.

The topic operationalises the EU-African Union partnership commitments on food systems, integrating Farm-to-Fork strategy ambitions with the African Union's Comprehensive Africa Agriculture Development Programme (CAADP) and the Africa Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA). Funded projects are expected to deliver actionable evidence for trade policymakers, certification bodies, and producer organisations on both continents.

Scope: Research and innovation actions are expected to (a) map and analyse current EU-AU agricultural and food trade flows, including value-chain segments most affected by climate and certification regimes; (b) study scaling pathways for climate-neutral food supply chains; (c) assess the climate, biodiversity and social impacts of cross-continental land-use changes driven by trade; (d) clarify the role of non-tariff measures, sanitary and phytosanitary standards, geographical indications, and private certification schemes in shaping fair trade outcomes; (e) co-design recommendations with African and European stakeholders.

Eligibility:
Minimum three African partners required (from at least two AU regions). Multi-actor approach mandatory. Standard Horizon Europe eligibility per Annex B applies for European partners. Joint Research Centre may participate.

Despesas elegiveis: pessoal de investigacao, viagens internacionais, fieldwork em paises africanos, dados, analise estatistica e econometrica, oficinas multi-stakeholder, comunicacao, dissemination, gestao de projeto. Maximum 20% EU funding allocation for financial support to third parties (60,000 per recipient maximum).

Criterios de selecao: relevancia para a parceria UE-AU em food systems, qualidade cientifica e metodologica, abordagem multi-actor, equilibrio EU-Africa no consortium, impacto esperado em politicas de comercio justo e clima-neutralidade, integracao de dimensoes sociais e de equidade.

Conditions: Standard Horizon Europe eligibility criteria apply per Work Programme General Annexes.

Keywords: Africa, Economics/Econometrics, Food and beverages, Food safety, Food traceability, International trade, Farm-to-Fork, EU-AU partnership, AfCFTA, climate-neutral supply chains.
