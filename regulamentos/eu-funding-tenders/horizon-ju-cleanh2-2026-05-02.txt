DESCRIPTION
This call focuses on developing odorants for hydrogen safety. Currently, natural gas uses odorisation per ISO16922 for safety. With the shift to 100% hydrogen in hard-to-abate industries, new risk mitigation strategies are essential.

Hydrogen requires different odorants than natural gas due to purity and physical property differences. New odorants must be non-toxic, environmentally benign, and provide sufficient olfactory warning with properties similar to those known for natural gas.

Project results should contribute to: providing odorant data to regulatory bodies; harmonised standards and guidelines; standard methods for hydrogen odorisation and de-odorisation.

SCOPE
Scope includes: data analysis from literature and prior research (Ready4H2, Hy4heat, Met4H2); testing odorants in hydrogen environments; chemical characterisation using GC-MS/MS, NMR, elemental analysis; stability and degradation analysis; toxicity assessment; absorption/permeation tests for polymers; performance impact on end-use systems; validated de-odorisation strategies; distribution network modelling; coordination with standardisation bodies and gas distributors.

The odorant should be detectable at 0.8% in air. Material compatibility testing (per ASME B31.12) and de-odorised hydrogen purity (per ISO 14687) are required.

EXPECTED OUTCOMES
"By 2030 hydrogen safety is understood and lived holistically and odorisation is one of the applied safety measures; By 2030 public awareness of odorised hydrogen is enhanced"
