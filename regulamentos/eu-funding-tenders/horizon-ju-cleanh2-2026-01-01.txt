DESCRIPTION
The EU promotes water reuse through regulatory frameworks to address scarcity pressures. New wastewater treatment directives mandate higher effluent quality and quaternary treatment in large facilities by 2045. Water electrolysis can leverage reclaimed water streams and advanced treatment effluents for hydrogen production, advancing both circular economy goals and renewable energy integration. However, robust technologies handling varied water qualities remain underdeveloped. Comprehensive understanding of impurity interactions with electrolysis systems is missing.

EXPECTED OUTCOMES
Projects should contribute to:
- Maintaining European leadership in hydrogen production with innovative embedded approaches utilizing high-quality effluents from advanced wastewater treatment
- Developing impurity-tolerant, durable electrocatalysts, membranes and components while minimizing critical raw materials (CRM) and perfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS)
- Identifying pre-treatment steps to minimize organic fouling and scaling
- Understanding cell performance, degradation and failure mechanisms with low-quality water feeds
- Contributing to EU harmonization protocols for water electrolysis

Key Performance Indicators:
- Electricity consumption: at or below 55 kWh/kg H2 (low-temp) or at or below 39 kWh/kg H2 (high-temp)
- Current density: at or above 1.0 A/cm2
- Degradation: at or below 1%/1000h
- CRM catalyst content: at or below 0.25 mg/W (PGM)
- Hydrogen purity: above 99.9%

SCOPE
Proposals must develop breakthrough water electrolysis technology producing hydrogen from low-quality water (beyond tap water, excluding saline/seawater) at low energy consumption. The prototype cell should incorporate innovative solutions at material, component, and cell architecture levels while minimizing PFAS and CRM use. Target validation at TRL 4.

Key Requirements:
- Determine Critical Maximum Concentration (CMC) of water impurities
- Identify deactivation/degradation mechanisms
- Develop and validate tolerant materials
- Conduct experimental and modeling studies
- Implement innovative monitoring techniques
- Validate KPIs at above 2kW scale for at least 2000 hours
- Identify wastewater application cases
- Compare metrics against conventional electrolysis
- Evaluate lifecycle, circularity and techno-economic feasibility
