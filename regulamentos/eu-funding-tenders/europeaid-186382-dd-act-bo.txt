EuropeAid/186382/DD/ACT/BO - Bolivia: Human Rights in the Mining Sector

Title: NDICI Human Rights - "Derechos Humanos en el Sector de la Minería en Bolivia"
Reference: EuropeAid/186382/DD/ACT/BO
Programme: NDICI Global Europe - Thematic Programme on Human Rights and Democracy 2021-2027 (Programa Europa Global)
Source: Government of Catalonia (gencat.cat), fundsforNGOs aggregator, EU Funding Portal listing
URL of original call page: https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/prospect/external/publishedcalls.htm?callId=186382

OVERVIEW
This EU call for proposals strengthens human rights and promotes sustainable, inclusive governance in Bolivia's mining sector. The action targets two critical extraction industries: gold mining (anywhere in Bolivia) and evaporitic resource extraction (lithium and brines) in the Potosí department. The call funds pilot projects and institutional capacity building to protect communities, indigenous peoples' rights and the environment in mining-affected areas, aligned with the EU Action Plan on Human Rights and Democracy 2025-2027.

BUDGET
- Total budget: EUR 1,300,000 (up to EUR 1.3 million envelope).
- Source: EU NDICI Global Europe Thematic Programme on Human Rights and Democracy.
- Programme: Europa Global 2021-2027 (NDICI-GE).

ELIGIBILITY
Lead applicant requirements:
- Non-profit legal entity.
- Civil society organisation (CSO) or thematic CSO network.
- Effectively established in one of the following:
  - An EU Member State.
  - A country with a Basic Framework Agreement (Acuerdo Marco Básico) with Bolivia.
  - Bolivia (legally established under Bolivian law).
- International organisations cannot be lead applicants.
- Must be directly responsible for action preparation and management, not merely an intermediary.

Co-applicants:
- At least one co-applicant is mandatory.
- Co-applicants from Bolivia, EU and other NDICI eligible countries are accepted.
- Indigenous peoples' organisations, women-led organisations and youth-led organisations are encouraged as co-applicants.

OBJECTIVES

Specific Objective 1: Strengthen access to justice in the mining sector
- Institutional mechanisms (due diligence systems, complaints mechanisms).
- Institutional capacity building of state and judicial actors.
- Legal empowerment of indigenous peoples and communities affected by mining.
- Transparency and accountability measures across the mining value chain.
- Civil society monitoring and oversight capacity.

Specific Objective 2: Measurable reduction of negative human rights and environmental impacts in mining
Implemented through two complementary pilot projects:
- Pilot 1: Gold extraction mining (any location in Bolivia), focused on artisanal and small-scale gold mining (ASGM) and the related supply chain.
- Pilot 2: Evaporitic resource extraction (lithium, brines, salt flats) specifically in the Potosí department.

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES
- Human rights due diligence implementation in mining operations and supply chains.
- Environmental protection mechanisms (water, soil, air quality monitoring).
- Community-led monitoring and impact documentation.
- Indigenous rights protection and free, prior and informed consent (FPIC) processes.
- Justice access mechanisms (legal aid, public interest litigation, access to remedy).
- Anti-criminal economy initiatives (combating illegal mining, money laundering linked to gold).
- Institutional capacity strengthening of state actors (Defensoría del Pueblo, Ministry of Mining, judicial system).
- Multi-stakeholder dialogues (government, communities, mining operators, financiers).
- Awareness raising on rights, complaint mechanisms and remedies.
- Support to women human rights defenders and indigenous peoples' organisations.

ELIGIBLE COSTS
Standard NDICI / EU external action cost categories apply:
- Personnel (Bolivian and international experts).
- Travel within Bolivia, including remote mining areas, and to relevant international fora.
- Equipment and supplies for monitoring activities.
- Sub-grants to local indigenous and community organisations (key delivery modality).
- Local office and operational costs.
- Communication, advocacy and visibility costs.
- Audit, monitoring and evaluation.
- Indirect costs at standard flat rate (up to 7 per cent).

DEADLINE
19 May 2026 (Brussels time).

EVALUATION CRITERIA
Standard EU concept note and full application scoring framework:
- Relevance to call objectives, the Bolivian mining context, and EU Action Plan on Human Rights and Democracy.
- Methodology and design, including pilot project quality and Theory of Change.
- Impact potential on human rights, environment and access to justice.
- Sustainability and ownership by Bolivian stakeholders, especially indigenous peoples and affected communities.
- Operational and financial capacity of the consortium.
- Cost effectiveness and budget realism.
- Cross-cutting themes: gender equality, conflict sensitivity, do-no-harm.

GEOGRAPHIC SCOPE
- National scope for governance and access to justice activities.
- Pilot 1: gold mining areas across Bolivia.
- Pilot 2: Potosí department (lithium / evaporitic resources, including the Salar de Uyuni and Coipasa salt flats).

PROGRAMME CONTEXT
- Part of the EU's Action Plan on Human Rights and Democracy 2025-2027.
- Aligned with the EU's Critical Raw Materials Act and responsible sourcing of strategic minerals (lithium for the EU green and digital transitions).
- Complements the EU-Bolivia bilateral cooperation under NDICI Global Europe Multi-Annual Indicative Programme 2021-2027.
- Supports the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights (UNGPs) and the OECD Due Diligence Guidance for Responsible Supply Chains of Minerals from Conflict-Affected and High-Risk Areas.
- Aligned with ILO Convention 169 on Indigenous and Tribal Peoples.
