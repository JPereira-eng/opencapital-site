## Complete Description

This action funds development of cost-effective wave and tidal energy systems operating with minimal environmental impact. Work spans three challenge areas:

Breakthrough Challenge: Developing new solutions and knowledge to accelerate ocean energy competitiveness, with technologies validated by project end. This includes early-stage exploratory work on novel concepts that may transform the wave and tidal sectors.

Validation Challenge: Testing technologies in laboratory and relevant environments, including both newly developed and previously created solutions. Validation work targets bridging the gap between lab prototypes and operational deployment readiness.

Demonstration Challenge: Conducting demonstration activities up to TRL 7, selected using stage gate approach methodology with defined go/no-go milestones. Demonstration projects must show real-world performance, durability and grid integration capability.

Key technical areas include:
- Innovative wave and tidal energy devices and power take-off systems
- Sustainable materials reducing environmental footprint and lifecycle costs
- Mooring systems for harsh marine environments with extended service life
- Condition monitoring with AI and digital twin applications for predictive maintenance
- Advanced manufacturing techniques enabling cost reduction and production scaling
- Installation and decommissioning technologies, including specialized vessels and procedures

Activities combine direct consortium implementation with Financial Support to Third Parties (FSTP) calls. The combination of direct R&I and cascading FSTP grants allows the action to support a broad community of ocean energy developers, SMEs, research organisations and demonstrator hosts.

Project duration: approximately 5 years.

Strong governance is required, including an Advisory Board with representatives from the SET Plan Implementation Working Group on Ocean Energy. Stage gate metrics for progress monitoring and clear go/no-go decision points are mandatory for demonstration activities.

A minimum of three demonstration activities with distinct focus areas (e.g., different device classes, deployment environments, or value-chain elements) must be planned. Comprehensive risk mitigation plans are required, covering technological, environmental, regulatory and commercial risks. Annual deliverables must align with SET Plan KPIs and contribute to ocean energy roadmap monitoring.

## Conditions

Page Limit: 60 pages (increased due to complexity).

Financial Support to Third Parties: Maximum 800,000 EUR per third party recipient.

Demonstration Budget Cap: 24 million EUR maximum EU funding for demonstration activities within a single project.

Project Management: Cannot exceed 7% of total EU funding.

Key Requirements:
- Strong governance with Advisory Board including SET Plan representatives
- Stage gate metrics for progress monitoring
- Three demonstration activities minimum with distinct focus areas
- Comprehensive risk mitigation plans
- Annual deliverables aligned with SET Plan KPIs

Standard Horizon Europe conditions apply: Annex A (admissibility), Annex B (eligible countries), Annex C (financial capacity), Annex D (award criteria), Annex F (evaluation processes), Annex G (legal and financial set-up). Granting authority may object to ownership transfer up to 4 years after end of action.

## Despesas Elegiveis e Criterios de Selecao
Despesas elegiveis: personnel costs, subcontracting, equipment, materials, FSTP grants up to 800,000 EUR per third party, project management up to 7% of EU funding. Criterios de selecao per Annex D: excellence, impact, quality and efficiency of implementation. TRL 7 by project end for demonstration activities. Stage gate approach with go/no-go milestones required. Three demonstration activities minimum with distinct focus.
