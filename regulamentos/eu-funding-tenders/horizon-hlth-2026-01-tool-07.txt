## Expected Outcomes

The initiative targets four primary outcomes:

1. The EU translates strategic priorities into implementable action plans, demonstrating technological and innovation leadership globally within life sciences.

2. Europe enhances its capacity, accessibility, coordination of its Advanced Therapies Medicinal Products (ATMP) infrastructures.

3. The European economy benefits from accelerated ATMP innovation development and commercialization within EU territories.

4. Patients across the EU gain expedited access to innovative ATMPs through augmented public research investment, harmonized policies, and strategies supporting healthcare system integration.

## Scope

### Context
ATMPs represent breakthrough medical treatments including gene therapies, cellular therapies, and tissue-engineered products. The European landscape contains 28 authorized products with numerous candidates in development. However, Europe global ATMP market share remains limited despite the Draghi competitiveness report emphasizing need for increased and focused public R&D investment.

### Core Objective
Establish a coordinated European network comprising no more than 10 CoEs across the EU, with complementary expertise in the various ATMP technologies.

### Centre Requirements
Each Centre of Excellence must:
- Operate as an existing facility within an active biocluster (pharmaceutical companies, research institutes proximity)
- Possess critical infrastructure for lab-to-patient advancement
- Include Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) and clinical trial facilities
- Execute comprehensive life sciences research spanning discovery through clinical application
- Demonstrate established field leadership through independent research programs

### Stakeholder Involvement
Networks should include multiple stakeholders beyond the research community, incorporating Member State ministries, regional authorities, funders, regulators, healthcare payers, industry actors, patient organizations, and policymakers.

### STEP Alignment
Activities must demonstrate contribution to either: (i) introducing innovative, economically promising technologies to the internal market, or (ii) reducing European strategic dependencies in advanced therapies.

## Expected Activities (2027-2030)

Proposals must address minimum requirements:

1. Needs Assessment & Policy Development: Identify shared ATMP R&D challenges; develop recommendations addressing clinical trials, manufacturing, logistics, regulatory (including harmonisation of market access authorisation and reimbursement procedures), public acceptance, policies for transnational care.

2. Strategic Roadmap Creation: Construct a pathway establishing Europe as the global ATMP leader by 2035, including clearly defined milestones, targets and Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), aligned with national plans and long-term funding strategies.

3. Advisory Board Formation: Establish a diverse stakeholder forum providing guidance and ensuring maximum utility of the generated outputs.

4. Education & Outreach: Develop training curricula for emerging scientists; implement public awareness activities and stakeholder facility access communication programs.

### Building on Prior Work
Proposals should reference experiences from RESTORE, Join4ATMP, PRECISEEU, and T2EVOLVE projects; coordinate with relevant European partnerships (Rare Diseases, Personalised Medicine) and the Innovative Health Initiative Joint Undertaking.
