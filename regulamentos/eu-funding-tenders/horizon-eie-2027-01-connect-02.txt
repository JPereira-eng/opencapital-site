## Description

This topic addresses the gap between innovation supply and public sector demand by strengthening connections between testing environments and procurement activities. The initiative enables innovators to develop and validate solutions alongside public buyers, test beds, living labs, and regulatory bodies. Consortia of public purchasers with shared procurement needs collaborate to stimulate market-driven innovation while removing regulatory and certification obstacles. The approach supports the EU Startup Scaleup Strategy and European Innovation Act by creating pathways for companies, especially startups, to reach public markets and scale operations. Solutions are expected to demonstrate significant innovation ambition and deployment scope.

Public buyers, test beds, living labs, regulatory authorities and certification bodies form an integrated ecosystem within which innovators can co-develop, demonstrate and scale technologies. By aligning the experimentation cycle with the procurement cycle, the topic shortens the path from early-stage testing to market uptake by public administrations. Beneficiaries are expected to mobilise consortia of public purchasers in different countries with common needs and to issue a joint pre-commercial procurement, distributing development risk and increasing demand-side aggregation.

The approach is intended to create new market-creating innovations across sectors such as health, mobility, environment, energy, digital infrastructure and security. Special attention is given to startups and SMEs, which often lack the capacity to navigate complex public procurement procedures. Funded projects should pilot new mechanisms to reduce procurement friction, harmonise specifications, and ensure that solutions developed under the project are interoperable with existing or planned public services.
