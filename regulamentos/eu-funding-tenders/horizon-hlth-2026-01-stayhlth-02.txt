## Full Description

### Expected Outcomes
The topic supports activities enabling impacts within Staying healthy in a rapidly changing society. Proposals should deliver results contributing to these outcomes:

- Healthcare professionals accessing behavioural interventions establishing and reinforcing healthy habits and sustain behavioural changes.
- Health professionals and educators obtaining evidence-based strategies to mitigate risks of Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs) for youth, with clear metrics.
- Youth gaining increased responsibility through targeted education, digital services, and community support involving healthcare professionals, educators, and families.
- Researchers accessing Real-World Data, health infrastructure, and digital tools including Artificial Intelligence supporting intervention success.
- Policymakers at multiple governance levels obtaining knowledge on behavioural interventions enabling improved approaches across diverse European contexts.

### Scope
The topic focuses on behavioural interventions for youth (ages 12-25) for primary prevention of prevalent NCDs (excluding cancer). Implementation research should deploy evidence-based interventions emphasizing empowerment and self-management (e.g. health literacy, health education, health promotion).

Proposals must include user-friendly hardware and software for self-monitoring (wearables, point-of-care devices, apps), with interoperable systems following international standards.

Proposals should integrate:
- Gender-sensitive and intersectional approaches addressing discrimination risks across cultural and socioeconomic backgrounds.
- Clear evidence-based deployment strategies at individual, family, community, and societal levels.
- Robust evaluation frameworks (randomized controlled trials, quasi-experimental designs) with defined success indicators.
- Collaboration formats involving healthcare professionals, educators, families, and policymakers.
- Plans for longer-term follow-up estimating health impact and cost savings.
- Policy integration considering diverse contexts and replication potential.
- Social sciences and humanities discipline expertise.
- FAIR data principles and GDPR-compliant practices.
- Clinical study details using provided templates.
