Description:

Next-generation tactical military networks leverage software-defined radio and network solutions to deliver secure, resilient communications resistant to cyberattacks. These modern approaches offer greater agility and flexibility than conventional methods, facilitating easier integration of mobile devices and cutting-edge technologies into military networks, including quantum key distribution, post-quantum cryptography, and physical unclonable functions. Quantum key distribution specifically enhances information-sharing trust and cyber defence by detecting intrusions and hacking attempts in real time using quantum principles.

Building upon prior EDF R&D efforts, this topic pursues development of software-defined network technologies and solutions creating a network architecture framework leveraging quantum technologies, providing EU armed forces with technological advantages in secure tactical networks.

Beyond defence applications, this topic advances STEP objectives within deep and digital technologies investment areas.
