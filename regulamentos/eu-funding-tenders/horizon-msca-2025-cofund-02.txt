CALL: HORIZON-MSCA-2025-COFUND-02
NAME: MSCA Choose Europe for Science 2025
PROGRAMME: Horizon Europe - Marie Sklodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA)
MANAGED BY: REA (Research Executive Agency)
SHARD: eu-horizon
DETECTED: 2026-04-26

DESCRIPTION:
The MSCA Choose Europe for Science pilot scheme aims to increase the attractiveness of European research careers by addressing precarity and offering excellent working conditions, converting the challenge of brain drain in the R&I sector into brain gain for Europe.

Choose Europe for Science supports projects in which academic and non-academic organisations recruit postdoctoral researchers with a vision to employment beyond the project period. Organisations are selected based not only on the research programme but also on their offers of longer-term professional perspectives for recruited researchers.

OBJECTIVES:
- Address precarity in European research careers
- Offer excellent, competitive working conditions for postdoctoral researchers
- Strengthen European research attractiveness versus global competitors
- Generate brain gain: attract and retain top international researchers in Europe
- Promote employment pathways beyond the fellowship period

TOPIC:
HORIZON-MSCA-2025-COFUND-02-01: MSCA Choose Europe for Science
Supporting programmes led by higher education institutions, research centres, regional authorities, funding agencies, and organisations managing postdoctoral programmes.

BUDGET:
Total call budget: EUR 22.5 million
Maximum per beneficiary: EUR 3.5 million

DEADLINE:
Application deadline: 3 December 2025, 17:00 CET
(Note: This deadline has passed as of April 2026. Call is closed.)

ELIGIBILITY:
- Legal entities established in EU Member States or Horizon Europe Associated Countries
- Eligible organisations: higher education institutions, research centres, regional authorities, national funding agencies, other organisations managing doctoral or postdoctoral programmes
- Single beneficiary or small consortium

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES:
- Recruitment of postdoctoral researchers (international mobility focus)
- Provision of competitive salaries and working conditions
- Training and professional development activities
- Mentoring and supervision support
- Career development pathways towards longer-term employment
- Research projects within the hosting organisation's research agenda

ELIGIBLE EXPENSES:
- Personnel costs for recruited postdoctoral researchers
- Training and career development costs
- Research-related expenses
- Overhead (indirect costs)

SELECTION CRITERIA:
- Quality of the hosting organisation's research environment
- Commitment to beyond-project employment perspectives
- Competitive working conditions offered
- Diversity and inclusion measures
- International recruitment strategy

CONTEXT:
This call is part of the broader European Commission effort to make Europe more competitive in attracting and retaining scientific talent. It complements standard MSCA COFUND (which co-funds doctoral and postdoctoral programmes) with a specific emphasis on employment stability.
