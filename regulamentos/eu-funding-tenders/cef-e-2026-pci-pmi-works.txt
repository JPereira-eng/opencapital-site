Deadline Date: 2026-10-01 (timestamp 1790726400000)

Description:

Expected Impact:
Financial support aims to advance Projects of Common Interest (PCIs) and Projects of Mutual Interest (PMIs) toward achieving TEN-E and CEF energy objectives: furthering internal energy market integration and competitiveness, ensuring cross-border network interoperability, facilitating economic decarbonization, promoting efficiency, and securing supply.

This initiative supports European Green Deal objectives alongside Paris Agreement commitments and 2030 climate targets, focusing on long-term decarbonization goals.

Expected Outcome:
Support enables PCI and PMI implementation within trans-European energy networks. The call backs infrastructure projects delivering substantial socio-economic benefits and Member State solidarity while lacking adequate market financing.

Supported projects advance Green Deal and Paris Agreement objectives plus 2030 climate targets. Financial assistance maximizes decarbonization value. The European Grids Package emphasizes infrastructure's critical role in energy transitions.

Objective:
Support and contribute to PCI and PMI implementation.

Scope:
This addresses projects conducting works contributing to the implementation of a PCI or a PMI. Works encompass component purchasing, supply, deployment, systems and services including software, development, construction, installation of eligible infrastructure, installation acceptance, and project launching.

Only projects on the Second Union PCI/PMI list qualify for EU grant support.

Eligibility Criteria:

Under TEN-E Regulation Article 18(2)-(3), certain PCIs/PMIs qualify for works grants if meeting all criteria:
- Project cost-benefit analyses show significant positive externalities including security of supply, system flexibility, solidarity or innovation
- Projects received cross-border cost allocation decisions or, for hydrogen projects outside national regulatory authority scope, provide cross-border services, bring technological innovation, and ensure safe grid operation
- Market financing and regulatory frameworks cannot support the project per business plans and investor/creditor assessments

Article 18(4) PCIs/PMIs qualify where promoters demonstrate significant positive externalities generated by the projects, such as security of supply, system flexibility, solidarity or innovation, and prove commercial non-viability through cost-benefit analyses and business plans.

Cyprus/Malta derogation projects (Article 24) must ensure future energy market access, prevent natural gas asset lifetime extension, guarantee cross-border interoperability, and demonstrate hydrogen conversion roadmaps by 2036.

Supporting documentation demonstrating externalities, cross-border service provision, technological innovation, grid safety, or commercial non-viability undergoes evaluation assessment. Incomplete or invalid submissions may lose eligibility.
