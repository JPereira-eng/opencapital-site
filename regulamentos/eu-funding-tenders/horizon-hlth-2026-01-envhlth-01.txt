## Expected Outcomes

The topic aims to ensure citizens, patients, public authorities, social care services, healthcare practitioners and policymakers have a better understanding of climatic health risks and enhanced prevention capabilities. Additionally, governments, public health authorities, researchers and civil society organisations are supported to tackle societal challenges linked to health impacts of climatic factors. Proposals should enable public authorities, organisations and the research community to rely on data collection and sharing according to FAIR principles. Finally, policymakers and public authorities develop evidence-based climate change and health policies and interventions.

## Scope

This topic addresses climate change as a health threat, particularly affecting vulnerable populations. Applicants must select one of three broad focus areas:

1. Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs) and individual safety: Exploring complex interactions between climate change and NCDs, which often involve multiple climate exposure pathways and compound and cascading climatic events.
2. Mental health and brain health: Increasing evidence on acute and long-term impacts of climate change and understanding of new syndromes related to climate stress.
3. Infectious diseases: Increasing understanding of factors driving climate-related burden from infectious diseases.

Proposals should employ a One Health approach where relevant.

## Expected Activities

Research actions should include multiple activities depending on relevance:
- Increase the understanding of correlations, causal pathways and mechanistic effects between climate change and disease/health outcomes.
- Develop longitudinal studies to better ascertain differential effects of climatic stressors on health.
- Study health impacts on vulnerable, sensitive or exposed population groups.
- Advance the knowledge on climate, ecological and environmental drivers of pathogen abundance.
- Explore the role of climate-driven human and wildlife mobility in enhancing global spread of pathogens.
- Increase the availability, accessibility, quality and standardisation of diagnostic testing.
- Increase the understanding of factors that strengthen health resilience to climate change.

International cooperation with low- and middle-income countries (LMICs) is strongly encouraged. All funded projects must participate in common networking and joint cluster activities with approximately 2% of total budget allocated for this purpose.
