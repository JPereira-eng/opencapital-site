## Complete Description

Expected Outcome:

Proposals must address all following points: improving cost and recovery efficiency of existing scaled recycling technologies; demonstrating technological solutions capable of efficiently recycling diverse feedstocks including production scrap and mixed lithium-ion battery chemistries; improving European competitiveness by reducing EU dependence on imported raw materials through enhanced recycling; demonstrable compliance with EU Batteries Regulation targets including recycling efficiency and material recovery rates.

Scope:

Projects must enhance adaptability, flexibility, and operational efficiency of existing battery recycling processes. Work explicitly targets improvements for processing mixed input streams, particularly production scrap and various lithium-ion battery chemistries. Proposals must demonstrate technical innovations in black mass processing achieving efficient recovery of high-purity battery-grade materials for reintroduction into European battery manufacturing.

Required elements include: developing recycling technologies handling variable composition feedstocks within same batches; optimizing hydrometallurgical and/or pyrometallurgical processes for enhanced critical raw metals recovery meeting EU Batteries Regulation requirements; detailed techno-economic assessments and comprehensive life-cycle assessments demonstrating significant improvements in energy efficiency, CO2 and ecological footprint reduction, and economic viability.

Proposals should include robust digitalized sorting and advanced pre-treatment technologies where required. The Commission's Safe and Sustainable by Design framework should serve as reference for assessing chemical and material safety and sustainability.

Exploitation plans must include preliminary scalability, commercialization, and deployment strategies (feasibility studies, business plans) indicating potential funding sources including the Innovation Fund.

Proposals may involve the European Commission's Joint Research Centre providing added value regarding battery circularity and sustainability aspects. The JRC can participate as consortium member with zero funding or as associated partner, without participating in proposal preparation.

Activities must achieve TRL 6-7 by project end. Projects will report results to the European Partnership on Batteries supporting KPI monitoring.

## Conditions

General conditions include: admissibility standards per Annexes A and E of Horizon Europe Work Programme General Annexes; proposal page limits and layout per Application Form Part B; eligible country requirements per Annex B; satellite-based earth observation requiring Copernicus and/or Galileo/EGNOS use; JRC participation provisions; financial and operational capacity assessments per Annex C; evaluation and award criteria per Annex D; submission and evaluation processes per Annexes F and Online Manual; evaluation timeline per Annex F; legal and financial setup per Annex G allowing granting authority objection to ownership transfer or exclusive licensing within 4 years post-action.

## Despesas Elegiveis e Criterios de Selecao
Despesas elegiveis: personnel costs, subcontracting, equipment depreciation, travel, consumables, indirect costs (standard Horizon Europe rules). Criterios de selecao per Annex D: excellence, impact, quality and efficiency of implementation. TRL 6-7 must be achieved by project end. Proposals must demonstrate compliance with EU Batteries Regulation targets.
