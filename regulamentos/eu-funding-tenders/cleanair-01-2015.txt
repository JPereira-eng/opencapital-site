Title: Horizon Prize - Materials for clean air
Framework Programme: Horizon 2020
Programme Division: Industrial Leadership (LEIT) - Nanotechnologies
Publication Date: April 16, 2015
Status: Closed (21 proposals submitted)

Budget
EUR 3,000,000 (allocated in 2015)

Deadline
Opening Date: January 26, 2017
Submission Deadline: January 23, 2018 (single-stage submission)

Description and Scope
This inducement prize addresses air pollution's severe health impacts. The EU estimates average life expectancy decreased by 8.6 months due to particulate matter from human activities. The prize challenges innovators to develop the best innovative design-driven material solution to reduce the concentration of particulate matter in urban areas.

Objective: Reducing particulate matter pollution through innovative material solutions that are design-driven, affordable, and sustainable. Vehicle exhaust systems are explicitly excluded from the scope of this prize.

The prize specifically targets material solutions that can be integrated into buildings, street furniture, or urban infrastructure to actively capture or prevent formation of particulate matter in the atmosphere. Solutions should function effectively across varying weather conditions typical of European urban environments.

Expected Results
Applicants must deliver affordable, sustainable and innovative design-driven material solutions that can effectively remove or prevent the formation of particulate matter in the atmosphere, functioning in their target environments.

Key Performance Requirements
- Demonstrable reduction in PM2.5 or PM10 concentration in the target area
- Affordable production and installation costs
- Long service life with minimal maintenance requirements
- Scalability to other urban environments
- Environmental safety of the materials themselves

Eligibility and Conditions
Detailed eligibility criteria, country-specific funding rules, and exclusion criteria are found in Part 8 of the Rules for Contest. Applicants must comply with geographic funding provisions and general Horizon 2020 participation requirements. Open to legal entities from any country.
