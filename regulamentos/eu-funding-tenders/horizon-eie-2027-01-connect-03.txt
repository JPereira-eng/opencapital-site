## Description

This initiative targets the integration of philanthropic organizations into Europe's research and innovation systems. The funding mechanism recognizes that Europe hosts over 33,000 philanthropic entities controlling approximately €567 billion in assets, with annual distributions exceeding €50 billion. Despite these substantial resources and longstanding collaborations between philanthropies and higher education institutions in research development, significant barriers persist in translating academic innovations into commercially viable solutions.

The action prioritizes bridging this innovation-to-market gap by leveraging philanthropic support beyond financial contributions. Participating foundations can provide access to investor networks, enhance stakeholder awareness, expedite technology adoption, and serve as initial users for testing emerging solutions. These roles extend the traditional philanthropic mission into active ecosystem participation.

Higher education institutions proposing under this scheme must articulate comprehensive commercialization strategies detailing engagement mechanisms with philanthropic organizations and venture capital investors. Plans should address early-stage company support, technology adoption by established businesses, and involvement of supplementary stakeholders facilitating market entry. Geographic scope encompasses regional, national, and European-level cooperation.

Eligible philanthropic organizations must satisfy the Philea public-benefit foundation definition, requiring five core characteristics: independent non-profit status, absence of members or shareholders, established income sources typically from endowments, independent governance boards, and distribution of resources for educational, cultural, religious, social, or other public-benefit purposes either through institutional support or direct programming.

Project results should generate measurable outcomes including enhanced collaboration among philanthropies, higher education, technology transfer offices, innovative enterprises, and venture capital providers; and expedited transition of academic research into market-ready solutions.

Concrete deliverables may encompass one or multiple activities: collaborative technology development with demonstrated application interest from startups, scaleups, or corporate entities; experimentation spaces including living labs, testing environments, incubators, or accelerators; supported spin-off launches securing venture funding from angel investors, accelerators, venture capital firms, corporate investors, philanthropic organizations, or national promotional banks; and accelerator development attracting startup cohorts toward venture funding. Projects typically span one to three years, permitting sufficient time for meaningful results achievement.

The Commission intends to support institutional capacity-building by launching a research effort mapping and profiling European philanthropic organizations engaged in research and innovation, facilitating identification of compatible partners for collaboration.

## Conditions

**Admissibility Requirements:** Proposal formatting and page limits follow specifications in Annex A and Annex E of the Horizon Europe Work Programme General Annexes, with detailed layout requirements in the Application Form available through the Submission System.

**Eligible Countries:** Detailed in Annex B of the Work Programme General Annexes. Non-EU and non-Associated Countries with specific funding arrangements may participate under conditions outlined in the Horizon Europe Programme Guide.

**Consortium Requirements:** The project coordinator must be a higher education institution or research technology organization(s). Consortia must include minimum three participants from three different member states or associated countries, encompassing higher education institutions or research technology organizations plus philanthropic organization(s) or networks thereof. The Joint Research Centre may participate as zero-funded consortium member or associated partner but cannot participate in proposal preparation.

**Financial and Operational Capacity:** Assessment criteria described in Annex C of the Work Programme General Annexes.

**Evaluation Criteria and Scoring:** Detailed in Annex D of the Work Programme General Annexes.

**Submission and Evaluation Processes:** Described in Annex F and the Online Manual.

**Grant Structure:** Contributions utilize lump sum methodology as defined in the July 7, 2021 Decision authorizing lump sum contributions under Horizon Europe. Full specifications appear in Annex G of the Work Programme General Annexes.
