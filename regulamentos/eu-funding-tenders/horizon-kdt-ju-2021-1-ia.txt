Research Focus Areas
Topics span key electronic components and systems technologies including:
- Smart factories and manufacturing automation
- Semiconductor electronics and advanced packaging
- Photonics applications and integration
- Integrated circuits design and fabrication
- Cyber-physical systems for industrial applications
- Robotics for manufacturing and service
- Networking technologies for Industry 4.0
- Embedded systems for automotive and aerospace
- Energy-efficient electronics and power management
- Medical electronics and health monitoring devices

Objectives
Innovation Actions under KDT JU 2021 aim to demonstrate and validate electronic components and systems technologies closer to market readiness. Unlike RIA actions, IA focuses on application demonstrations and pilot lines, with activities predominantly consisting of demonstration, testing, prototyping, piloting, and validation at scale.

Projects should demonstrate clear industrial take-up pathways and quantifiable benefits for European industry competitiveness. Cross-sectoral collaboration between electronic components suppliers and system integrators in strategic application domains (automotive, health, energy, communications) is encouraged.

Key Requirements
Applicants must consult the KDT JU Work Programme 2021 for detailed funding rates. Organisations must calculate funding according to the Guide for Applicants, as funding rates of large enterprises are not adequately integrated in standard budget tables. National budget tables require separate Excel file uploads. Minimum consortium requirements apply (typically 3 entities from 3 different member states).

Note: This call closed April 2022. National co-funding from participating member states complements EU contribution under this Joint Undertaking.
