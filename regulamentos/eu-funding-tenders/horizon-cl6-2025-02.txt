DESCRIPTION:
HORIZON-CL6-2025-02 is the second call of 2025 within Cluster 6 of Horizon Europe, which covers food systems, bioeconomy, natural resources, agriculture, and environment. This call uses primarily single-stage submission with some two-stage topics.

Cluster 6 is organized across seven thematic Destinations:
1. Biodiversity and ecosystem services
2. Fair, healthy and environmentally friendly food systems from primary production to consumption
3. Resilient, inclusive, healthy and green rural, coastal and urban areas
4. Innovative governance, environmental observations, and digital solutions in support of the Green Deal
5. Land, ocean and water for climate action
6. Accelerating the transition to a circular and sustainable bio-based sectors
7. A sustainable, resilient and competitive agri-food sector

SELECTED TOPICS IN HORIZON-CL6-2025-02 (single-stage):
- Overcoming the barriers for scaling up circular water management in agriculture
- Developing innovative phytosanitary measures for plant health
- Improving grassland management in European livestock farming systems
- Nutrients produced by microorganisms utilising primarily CO2 from the air

TWO-STAGE TOPICS:
Under HORIZON-CL6-2025-02-FARM2FORK and HORIZON-CL6-2025-02-CLIMATE:
Stage 1 deadline: 4 September 2025
Stage 2 deadline: 18 February 2026

SINGLE-STAGE DEADLINE:
16 September 2025, 17:00 CEST

BUDGET:
Total allocation across Cluster 6 2025 Work Programme: EUR 948.9 million.
Indicative per-project funding: EUR 3-18 million depending on topic and action type.

ELIGIBILITY:
- International consortia with significant participation of knowledge users (farmers, food industry, SMEs, public authorities)
- Established in EU Member States or Horizon Europe Associated Countries
- Typical consortium: 8-25 entities from EU and associated countries
- SMEs and farmers are encouraged to participate, especially in topics targeting practical implementation

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES:
- Research and Innovation Actions (RIA)
- Innovation Actions (IA) closer to market
- Coordination and Support Actions (CSA)
- Multi-actor projects bringing together researchers, farmers, advisory services, and industry
- Living labs and real-world testing environments

ELIGIBLE EXPENSES:
- Personnel costs
- Subcontracting costs
- Other direct costs (equipment, consumables, travel)
- Indirect costs (25% flat rate overhead)

SELECTION CRITERIA:
- Excellence: scientific quality and innovation level
- Impact: contribution to Green Deal, Farm to Fork Strategy, Biodiversity Strategy
- Quality and efficiency of implementation: consortium composition, work plan
