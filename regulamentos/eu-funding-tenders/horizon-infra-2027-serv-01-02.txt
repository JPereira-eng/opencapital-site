## FULL DESCRIPTION

**Expected Outcome:**

For all areas: Applicants should enable more efficient access to premier research infrastructures available to researchers and innovators conducting challenge-driven research. This includes improving responsiveness to emerging challenges and fostering innovation through a single-entry point access portal with integrated or interoperable service catalogues and convergent access conditions.

Breakthrough and leading-edge research should be enabled by advanced research infrastructure services, including joint research activities, made accessible to broader user communities across emerging research areas.

A new generation of researchers should be trained to optimally leverage essential research tools, with particular attention to early-stage career researchers and researchers from widening countries and candidate countries.

Cross-disciplinary fertilization should be fostered through closer interactions between researchers and innovators working within research infrastructures, encouraging cross-disciplinary joint research activities for customized services with appropriate research security considerations. Better management should implement FAIR data principles for continuous data flow collected or produced by research infrastructures.

**Scope:**

This topic provides trans-national access (on-site or remote) and/or virtual access to integrated and customized research infrastructure services for challenge-driven research and innovation, offered by complementary and interdisciplinary top-level research infrastructures. Proposals must adhere to the European Charter for Access to Research Infrastructures.

Access encompasses ad hoc user training and scientific and technical support. Additional training courses, including data stewardship skills, may support preparation of new researcher generations to exploit leading-edge infrastructures. Proposals should reserve sufficient resources and proactively advertise training opportunities arranged after user project selection.

The primary goal remains access provision to existing services, clearly reflected in proposed activities and allocated resources. Service improvement and development relevant to identified challenges will be supported, provided resulting services are opened and offered during the action with ensured long-term sustainability by participant research infrastructures. Further service development for mid-term use (2-3 years) may be supported when justified by identified needs, such as ESFRI Landscape Analysis recommendations or Horizon Europe Mission research agendas.

Data management, interoperability, and connection of digital services to the European Open Science Cloud should be addressed where relevant. Proposals should account for major European or international initiatives and EU priorities in relevant domains, fostering deployment of open global standards.

Proposals must make available to researchers a wide, inclusive, and comprehensive portfolio of complementary research infrastructure services of European interest, including data services and customized workflows enabling R&I addressing identified challenges. Proposals must include at least one ESFRI Landmark or European Research Infrastructure Consortium (ERIC) as beneficiary. For distributed ERICs, alternatively, a legal entity hosting ERIC facilities, resources, or related services may participate as beneficiary.

Access may extend to third-country researchers when appropriate. Research infrastructures from third countries may participate when demonstrating complementary or more advanced services than available through EU Member States and Associated Countries as beneficiaries or affiliated entities.

Proposals should include outreach and engagement plans actively advertising services to targeted research communities, particularly from widening countries and candidate countries, and to relevant industries including SMEs. Dedicated support for developing research partnerships and collaborations with researchers from widening countries and candidate countries should be provided. Synergies should be exploited and complementarity ensured with other EU grants supporting access provision.

Proposals must list services/installations opened by research infrastructures for trans-national or virtual access and specify available access unit amounts for users. Further conditions detailed in the "Specific features for Research Infrastructures" work programme section must be fulfilled.

**Six Thematic Areas (proposals must explicitly state which area addressed):**

**Area 1: Advancing cancer research through integrated biomedical infrastructures.** Actions should integrate complementary and interdisciplinary key research infrastructures supporting the full cancer research lifecycle, facilitating innovative treatment development including immunotherapies and personalized medicine. Services should encompass advanced imaging, genomics, and proteomics platforms, along with clinical trials management, addressing cancer heterogeneity and epigenetics. Links should be ensured with the Horizon Europe Mission on cancer, EU4Health, and other key European and international initiatives.

**Area 2: Sustainable and resilient agrifood systems and agroecological transitions contributing to EU policies with a One Health approach and fostering EU competitiveness.** Actions should integrate complementary key research infrastructures enhancing R&I in sustainable agrifood systems and agroecological transitions, consistent with the One Health approach and supporting EU policies and competitiveness. Big data approaches should be promoted through trans-national access to similar or complementary genetic resources across installations, adhering to FAIR principles, and developing AI and statistical applications.

**Area 3: Resilient and sustainable polar ecosystems: understanding and managing climate, biodiversity, and human activity interactions.** Proposals should provide access to wide service ranges supporting R&I in polar regions addressing key areas: climate change impacts, biodiversity, ecosystem management, pollution, tourism, geo-political changes, and impacts on indigenous and local communities. Services should be adaptable, allowing integration of new technologies and methodologies, promoting science diplomacy.

**Area 4: Increasing material circularity and improving materials' environmental performance.** Actions should integrate complementary and interdisciplinary key research infrastructures relevant for materials research and innovation for circularity and improved environmental performance, enabling Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation and Critical Raw Materials Act goals achievement. Services should address different Technology Readiness Levels, serving stakeholders across the whole value chain with possible industrial applications.

**Area 5: Semiconductors: sustainable development of next-generation and future semiconductor chips and related technologies.** In line with the European Chips Act, actions should provide research infrastructure services contributing to R&I strengthening manufacturing activities in the Union, stimulating the European design ecosystem, and supporting scale-up and innovation across the whole semiconductor value chain.

**Area 6: Geosphere research infrastructures for advanced research on geohazards driven by the dynamics of the Earth and their dependency on human-induced changes.** Actions should integrate complementary and interdisciplinary geosphere research infrastructures key for understanding Earth dynamics driving natural hazards (earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, landslides, tsunamis) and their dependency on human-induced changes (climate and exploration, storage, exploitation of resources). Strengthening science-policy-society interfaces should contribute to evidence-based decision-making.

## CONDITIONS AND REQUIREMENTS

**Admissibility:** Applicants are not required to include exploitation and dissemination plans, as the primary objective is service provision. Maximum page limit: 100 pages.

**Eligible Countries:** Described in Annex B of the Work Programme General Annexes. Select non-EU/non-Associated Countries with specific funding provisions are eligible; details are in the Horizon Europe Programme Guide.

**Other Eligible Conditions:** Subject to restrictions protecting European communication networks. The JRC may participate as a zero-funded beneficiary or associated partner. Access provision activities must be included in proposals. Legal entities established in Australia, Brazil, Canada, Chile, India, Japan, Mexico, New Zealand, Republic of Korea, Singapore, and USA providing state-of-the-art research infrastructure access to Member States and Associated Country researchers are exceptionally eligible for Union funding.

**Evaluation and Award Criteria:** To ensure balanced portfolio coverage across areas, grants will be awarded not only in ranking order but also to highest-ranked proposals within each area, provided applications meet all thresholds. For 'Excellence', additional consideration includes the extent to which access activities will offer access to state-of-the-art European-interest infrastructures, high-quality services, and enable excellent user research.

**Legal and Financial Set-up:** Beneficiaries shall grant royalty-free access to intellectual property relating to tools, standards, specifications, and other relevant outputs generated by the action to EU institutions and beneficiaries of EU-funded projects. Eligible costs may take the form of unit costs for trans-national and virtual research infrastructure access.
