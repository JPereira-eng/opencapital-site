Description and Scope
This inducement prize challenges participants to develop a rapid test that can identify at the point of care patients with upper respiratory tract infections that can safely be managed without antibiotics. Upper respiratory tract infections include pharyngitis, sinusitis, otitis, and bronchitis.

The prize targets the development of a test that is applicable in various care settings including general practice (GP) surgeries and out-of-hours centres, is easy to use without specialised training, gives a result within 20 minutes, is affordable in both high and low income countries, has high sensitivity and high specificity, and is stable without refrigeration.

Primary Objectives
- Reduce unnecessary antibiotic use in viral upper respiratory infections
- Decrease costs and side effects from antibiotic use
- Delay emergence of antibiotic-resistant organisms
- Enable healthcare providers to make informed decisions about treatment
- Address respiratory infections amid aging populations and rising healthcare costs

The challenge is set in context of growing antimicrobial resistance (AMR). Around 25,000 people die each year in the EU from infections with antibiotic resistant bacteria. The prize encourages development of diagnostic tools that distinguish bacterial from viral infections at point-of-care.
