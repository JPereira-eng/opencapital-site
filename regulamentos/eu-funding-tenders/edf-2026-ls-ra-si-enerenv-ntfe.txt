DESCRIPTION

Unmanned aerial vehicles deployed as unmanned loyal wingmen or combat systems require specialized performance across multiple mission profiles to satisfy storage, deployment, and operational demands. A critical need exists for engines delivering optimal thrust and electrical power for these applications.

This topic investigates a new turbofan engine approximately 25-35 kN in capacity meeting military requirements and capable of operating on kerosene, hydrogen, and sustainable aviation fuel.

The initiative supports STEP objectives in defence technology investment. Targeted activities include knowledge generation, knowledge integration, and design studies.

Proposals must build upon or integrate results from EU-funded projects emphasizing civil applications, with applicants holding necessary commercialization rights.

Several proposals may receive funding.
