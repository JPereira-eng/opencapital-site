HORIZON-CL4-2026-01-MAT-PROD-05
Circular innovative advanced materials: facilitating the transition from design to markets (RIA)

Call Identifier: HORIZON-CL4-2026-01
Framework Programme: Horizon Europe (HORIZON)
Programme Division: Global Challenges and European Industrial Competitiveness / Digital, Industry and Space (HORIZON.2.4)
Action Type: HORIZON Research and Innovation Actions (RIA)
MGA Type: HORIZON Lump Sum Grant (HORIZON-AG-LS)
Status: Open
Deadline: 2026-04-21

Budget Overview (2026):
- Total: 37.000.000 EUR
- Expected grants: 7 projects
- Funding range per project: 5.000.000 - 6.500.000 EUR

Description:
This initiative seeks proposals developing innovative advanced materials designed for circularity in recyclable polymers, composites, magnets, and metal alloys suitable for additive manufacturing. Work encompasses the full innovation cycle from design through industrial uptake, targeting strategic value chains in mobility and medical devices.

Key Scope Elements:
- Design for circularity and functional integration of advanced materials
- Development and scalable recovery/recycling processes
- Industrial demonstration and product integration (TRL 4-5 start, TRL 6 at conclusion)
- Circular business model development
- Digital tools and FAIR data adoption
- AI-driven methodologies for materials discovery
- Safe and Sustainable by Design (SSbD) frameworks
- Collaborative stakeholder engagement

Portfolio Approach:
Proposals should support strategic value chains in mobility and medical devices. At least one project from each of these two areas will be funded.

Conditions:
- Admissibility per Annexes A and E of Work Programme General Annexes
- Eligible countries per Annex B
- Financial capacity assessment per Annex C
- Evaluation per Annex D
- Lump sum grant model
- Budget justification required for cost categories C/D exceeding 15% of personnel costs (updated Feb 16, 2026)
- Start TRL 4-5; achieve TRL 6 by project end

Keywords: Advanced materials, circularity, recyclable polymers, composites, magnets, metal alloys, additive manufacturing, Safe and Sustainable by Design, digital tools, FAIR data
