EuropeAid/186235/DD/ACT/YE - Call for Proposals (Restricted) - Yemen Women Economic Empowerment

Title: Call for proposals (restricted) supporting economic empowerment of women in Yemen
Reference: EuropeAid/186235/DD/ACT/YE
Programme: EU Multiannual Support Programme for Yemen 2025-2027 (NDICI Global Europe - Yemen Country Allocation)
Source: EU Delegation Yemen + EEAS Yemen page
Original call URL: https://www.eeas.europa.eu/delegations/yemen/call-proposals-restricted-europeaid186235ddactye_en

OVERVIEW
The EU Delegation to Yemen has launched a restricted call for proposals to support economic empowerment of women in Yemen. The call mobilises an indicative envelope of EUR 5,000,000 under the EU Multiannual Support Programme for Yemen 2025-2027. The action targets job creation for women, financial inclusion of women-led enterprises, and integration of women-led businesses into EU and Middle East / Gulf supply chains.

GLOBAL OBJECTIVE
Support economic empowerment of Yemeni women through job creation, access to finance and integration into regional and international value chains, contributing to inclusive economic recovery and resilience in Yemen.

KEY DEADLINES AND CALENDAR
- Information session: 5 May 2026 (registration deadline 30 April 2026).
- Concept note submission deadline: 3 June 2026 at 12:00 (Brussels date and time).
- Two-stage process: restricted concept note first, full application requested only from shortlisted.

INDICATIVE BUDGET
Total envelope: EUR 5,000,000 (indicative).
Number of grants: typically 1-3 large grants, given the EUR 5M envelope and the 36-48 month duration.
Grant min/max: not publicly specified, but consistent with the EUR 5M envelope and 36-48 months duration, individual grants likely in the EUR 1.5M to EUR 5M range.

ELIGIBILITY (LEAD APPLICANTS)
- Non-governmental organisations (NGOs).
- Private sector operators (including chambers of commerce, business associations, social enterprises).
- International organisations.
- Local Yemeni organisations strongly encouraged.
- Non-Yemen-based organisations must partner with at least one local Yemeni organisation as co-applicant.
- Cannot apply as a natural person.

ELIGIBLE COUNTRIES OF ESTABLISHMENT
Yemen primarily. International applicants from EU 27, NDICI eligible countries, OECD/DAC list permitted, but partnership with Yemeni local actors required.

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES
- Job creation initiatives for women in viable economic sectors (agriculture, fisheries, services, light manufacturing).
- Financial inclusion: access to credit, microfinance, savings products for women-led enterprises.
- Trade and business linkages: women-led enterprises connecting to EU and Middle East / Gulf supply chains.
- Vocational training and skills development for women.
- Business development services and incubation for women entrepreneurs.
- Support to women-led cooperatives and producer associations.
- Policy and advocacy on women economic rights in Yemen.

ELIGIBLE COSTS
Standard NDICI Global Europe categories: staff, travel, equipment and supplies, local office, services, sub-granting (up to 30% of EU contribution permitted), audit, visibility/communication, indirect costs (flat rate up to 7%).

CO-FINANCING
EU contribution typically up to 95% of eligible costs given the fragile context of Yemen.

PROJECT DURATION
Minimum 36 months, maximum 48 months.

APPLICATION PROCESS
- Online submission via PROSPECT platform of the EU Funding & Tenders Portal mandatory.
- Restricted: only concept notes initially submitted by all interested applicants, full applications by invitation only.
- Languages: English.

EVALUATION CRITERIA
Concept notes: relevance to women economic empowerment in Yemen (35-40 points), design and methodology (20-25), feasibility and security context management (20), cost-effectiveness (15-20).

NOTES
This call complements the broader EU humanitarian and resilience portfolio in Yemen, where the EU is one of the largest donors. Operating context is fragile, security and access constraints require strong local partnerships. The focus on women economic empowerment aligns with the EU Gender Action Plan III and the EU Action Plan on Women, Peace and Security.
