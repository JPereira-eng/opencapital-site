DESCRIPTION
Earth System Models serve as primary assessment tools for climate change projection. Despite advances in resolution and realism, significant gaps remain in representing key processes, interactions, and feedbacks limiting prediction accuracy. Focus areas include global-regional climate linkages, extreme events, and water cycle variability.

EXPECTED OUTCOMES
Projects should advance:
- Understanding and predictive capability of Earth system evolution across spatial and temporal scales
- Regional climate variability prediction, particularly precipitation and extremes
- Collaborative cross-fertilization strengthening next-generation ESM development
- Long-term evidence base supporting European and international climate policy

SCOPE AND OBJECTIVES
Actions must address:

1. Enhanced simulation capability through improved representation of coupled Earth systems, including terrestrial-ocean interactions, climate-carbon-water feedbacks, aerosol-cloud forcing, and land use scenario impacts
2. Cross-model collaboration spanning various resolutions and fidelity levels
3. Data infrastructure development integrating observational datasets, reanalysis products, models, and validation tools

Projects should leverage machine learning and AI technologies while maintaining highest standards of transparency and openness regarding assumptions, protocols, and code management per FAIR principles.
