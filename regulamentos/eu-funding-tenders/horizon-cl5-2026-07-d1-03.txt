HORIZON-CL5-2026-07-D1-03
Economics of Climate Change and Cost of Inaction

CALL IDENTIFIER: HORIZON-CL5-2026-07
TOPIC: HORIZON-CL5-2026-07-D1-03
STATUS: Open
PROGRAM: Horizon Europe - Climate Science and Solutions (HORIZON.2.5.1)
ACTION TYPE: RIA (Research and Innovation Actions)
FUNDING MODEL: Lump sum grants

DEADLINE: 21 April 2026, 17:00 CET (postponed due to technical issues)
PUBLICATION DATE: 16 April 2026

BUDGET:
- This topic (D1-03): EUR 16,000,000
- Expected grants: 4
- Grant range: EUR 4,000,000 per project
- Total call (all D1 topics): EUR 82,000,000

OBJECTIVES:
The call seeks research on more robust understanding of the costs of climate inaction, including distribution of impacts, foregone benefits in health/biodiversity, and increased adaptation needs. Projects should develop enhanced models addressing gaps in current methodologies and integrate latest scientific evidence.

TWO PRIORITY AREAS:
Area A - Global analysis with low/middle-income country cooperation (minimum 3 independent entities from low or lower/upper-middle-income countries required)
Area B - EU-focused with emphasis on industrial performance and strategic autonomy

DESPESAS ELEGIVEIS / ELEGIBILIDADE:
- EU member states and associated countries (Annex B)
- Area A: minimum 3 independent entities from low or lower/upper-middle-income countries as beneficiaries or partners
- Open access required for new modules, models or tools developed from scratch or substantially improved
- Copernicus and/or Galileo/EGNOS mandatory for satellite data
- Interdisciplinary collaboration between physical scientists, economists, and social science disciplines required
- Stakeholder co-design process mandatory
- Lump sum grants - budget justification required for categories C/D exceeding 15% of personnel costs

CRITERIOS DE SELECAO:
Portfolio balance mechanism: grants awarded not only in ranking order but also to fund at least two highest ranked applications in each area.

KEYWORDS: Climate economics, cost of inaction, socio-economic impacts, climate adaptation, mitigation assessment, economic security, damage functions
