DIGITAL-2026-BESTUSE-RSF-10-AWARENESS
Research Support Framework for Situational Awareness on information integrity

Call Identifier: DIGITAL-2026-BESTUSE-RSF-10
Programme: Digital Europe Programme (2021-2027)
Type of Action: DIGITAL-GFS (Grants for Financial Support)
Deadline: 2026-10-02

Budget Overview:
- 2026: 6,000,000 EUR
- Expected Grants: 1 (consortium)

Note: Information session scheduled 28 April 2026, 15:00-16:30 CEST.

Keywords: New technologies for Audio-Visual sector and Media, Social Media, DSA, Digital Europe Programme, European Democracy Shield, Fact checkers, information integrity, media, social media platforms

Description:
The overarching objective is to create a common research framework with the necessary infrastructure to enable advanced research and analysis on information integrity in the EU. This action supports the European Democracy Shield and the Digital Services Act (DSA) framework.

Specific objectives:
1. Secure and set up necessary infrastructure (hardware and software) for advanced information integrity research accessible by third parties across the EU
2. Support, acquire, or create dedicated tools to facilitate information integrity research and analysis of information manipulation
3. Make tools and computational capacity accessible to researchers and civil society through a transparent access mechanism
4. Contribute to the DSA data access regime by collecting research community views on very large online platforms' data access APIs, documenting best practices and shortcomings
5. Set up and implement a grant structure to distribute funds to third parties from research and civil society communities, funding tools/capabilities and research projects utilizing established outputs

Grant for Financial Support structure:
- Delivery of actions by a consortium co-funded at 100%
- Distribution of majority of funds (at least 60%) to third parties
- Third party selection based on fair and transparent criteria

Key considerations:
- All examples in the call are indicative illustrations only
- Proposals should be based on evidence-informed needs analysis centered on research and civil society community demands
- Focus on quality over quantity
- Emphasis on long-term sustainability and longevity of infrastructure

Expected outcomes:
- Strong, visible, and recognised impact in scaling, facilitating, and accelerating research and analytical efforts on the information environment and information integrity
- Technical solutions created or supported for information integrity research
- Insights from supported research and monitoring projects
- Proof of concept and lessons learnt for shared research infrastructures
- Community of researchers and civil society organizations working on information integrity
