## Description

### Expected Outcomes
The topic aims at supporting activities that advance the destination "Maintaining an innovative, sustainable, and competitive EU health industry." Proposals should deliver results directed at:

1. Policymakers and regulators will get accelerated access to improved evidence driven methodologies for assessing novel health technologies and enabling uptake in clinical practice.

2. Patients and healthcare systems benefit from more targeted and efficient uptake of safe and effective health innovations in clinical practice, supporting more personalised approaches and improved care.

### Scope

The topic addresses gaps in regulatory science for health technologies across their lifecycle, from development through post-market surveillance. Key focus areas include:

Regulatory Science Updates: Improving delineation of requirements and incorporating novel biomedical approaches, including artificial intelligence (AI), virtual human twin, new approach methodologies, and digital solutions that model biological parameters and exploit novel biomarkers.

Technology Coverage: All health technologies including medical devices, in-vitro diagnostics, AI, and Substances of Human Origin (SoHO).

Regulatory Frameworks: Applicants must consider MDR, IVDR, the proposed SoHO-Regulation, and AI Act among others.

### Expected Activities

Proposals should address one or more:

1. Data and Analyses on Regulatory Science Refinement: Data and analyses on how existing approaches in regulatory science can be refined and improved in view of closing existing gaps of clarity, sufficiency of clinical evidence.

2. Novel Information Sources: Examine whether novel information sources from biomedicine including new approach methods and digital and AI-enabled models can contribute to clinical evaluation, including:
   - Providing information on biophysical, anatomical, physiological and other disease-relevant aspects.
   - Supporting information integration through the use and aggregation of already existing data including Real-World Data (RWD) from registries.
   - Enhancing planning and design of first-in-man clinical studies.

3. Special Populations and Pathways: Examine support for innovative technologies for unmet medical needs and for special patient populations (e.g. paediatric and rare conditions) via regulatory pathways or regulatory sandbox frameworks.

Exclusion: The actual conduct of clinical studies is not in scope of this topic.
