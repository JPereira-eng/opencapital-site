Description
This topic focuses on improving detection, tracking, and identification capabilities across large areas with extended range and minimal delay. The initiative builds on previous over-the-horizon (OTH) research to develop an EU concept for a cognitive and scalable network of HF OTH sensors.

Key elements include exploring both HF surface radar (active, cooperative, and non-cooperative passive) and skywave radar technologies for integration within an agile network supporting strategic surveillance and data sharing among EU member states.

Targeted Activities
- Studies into HF over-the-horizon radar network architectures
- Design work for cognitive radar systems with adaptive frequency management
- Upstream development of signal processing algorithms for OTH detection
- Downstream development toward prototype demonstration
- Integration studies for interoperability with existing surveillance systems
- Data fusion and command-and-control interface development

Objectives
Develop technologies for an EU OTH radar network providing deep cooperation and information exchange for strategic purposes, addressing gaps in existing surveillance capabilities. The action aims to reduce European dependence on non-EU surveillance systems for monitoring large maritime and territorial areas.

Expected outcomes include technology demonstrators for HF surface wave radar, feasibility assessments for skywave OTH radar, architecture concepts for networked OTH sensor systems, and validated signal processing techniques for challenging propagation environments.
