EuropeAid/186141/DD/ACT/Multi - African Governance, Peace and Security Architectures (Grant Component)

Title: Support to the African Governance, Peace and Security Architectures - grant component
Reference: EuropeAid/186141/DD/ACT/Multi
Programme: NDICI Global Europe / Pan-African Programme (PANAF) - EU External Action PROSPECT
Source: DevelopmentAid grant listing and EEAS Delegation to the African Union announcement
URL of original call page: https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/prospect/external/publishedcalls.htm?callId=186141

OVERVIEW
This EU action grant reinforces the nexus between the African Union Governance Architecture (AGA) and the African Peace and Security Architecture (APSA), with specific attention to conflict prevention, citizen engagement (in particular youth and women) and AU Member State compliance with AU Continental Shared Values on Governance, Peace and Security. The action is the grant component of a wider multi-instrument support package to the African Union under the EU-AU Partnership.

BUDGET AND GRANT SIZE
- Total budget: EUR 4,000,000.
- Award range per grant: EUR 1,900,000 to EUR 2,000,000.
- Number of grants expected: 2.
- Funding agencies: European Commission Directorate-General for International Partnerships (INTPA) and African Union Commission.

ELIGIBILITY
Eligible applicants:
- Non-profit legal persons (NGOs, civil society organisations, foundations).
- Legally registered CSO networks and platforms.
- Public sector operators including research centres and academia and universities.
- Government and public bodies operating without profit.

Geographic eligibility:
- Lead applicants must be effectively established in an EU Member State or in an African Union Member State.
- Eligible nationalities: EU 27 countries plus 52 African Union Member States.

PARTNER REQUIREMENTS
- Co-applicant participation expected.
- Partnerships between EU-based organisations and African continental or regional networks are encouraged.
- Specific encouragement for inclusion of youth-led and women-led organisations as co-applicants.

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES
- Strengthening the AGA-APSA nexus through joint analysis, policy advocacy and operational coordination.
- Citizen engagement initiatives, in particular for youth and women, on AU governance, peace and security agendas.
- Monitoring and reporting on AU Member State compliance with AU Continental Shared Values (African Charter on Democracy, Elections and Governance; African Peer Review Mechanism; Silencing the Guns agenda).
- Support to AU early warning systems and conflict prevention mechanisms involving civil society.
- Promotion of the Women, Peace and Security agenda and Youth, Peace and Security agenda at continental and regional level.
- Support to dialogue between AU Commission, Regional Economic Communities (RECs) and civil society.
- Capacity building of African civil society networks engaging with AU institutions.

SECTORS AND OBJECTIVES
- Gender and Human Rights.
- Disaster Reduction and Humanitarian Relief.
- Peace, security and governance, conflict prevention and stabilisation.

ELIGIBLE COSTS
Standard NDICI / EU external action cost categories:
- Personnel costs (international and local).
- Missions and travel within Africa and to Brussels.
- Sub-granting to African CSOs (where allowed by the call).
- Equipment and supplies (limited).
- Communication, advocacy and visibility costs.
- Office and operational running costs.
- Audit, evaluation and monitoring.
- Indirect costs at the standard flat rate (up to 7 per cent of direct eligible costs).

DEADLINE
- Original deadline: 30 April 2026.
- Extended deadline (per Corrigendum n.1): 7 May 2026, 16:00 (Brussels time).

PUBLISHED
16 March 2026.

LANGUAGE
English.

EVALUATION CRITERIA
Standard EU external action evaluation framework applies:
- Relevance to call objectives, AU Continental Shared Values and AGA-APSA priorities.
- Methodology, work plan and quality of proposed governance and conflict prevention activities.
- Sustainability and replicability of results.
- Operational and financial capacity of the lead applicant and co-applicants.
- Cost effectiveness and budget realism.
- Cross-cutting themes (gender, youth, human rights, do-no-harm).

PROGRAMME CONTEXT
- The action complements the African Peace Facility (APF) and the European Peace Facility (EPF) instruments under the EU-AU Partnership.
- Aligned with the AU Agenda 2063, the AU Master Roadmap of Practical Steps to Silence the Guns in Africa, the Maputo Protocol on the Rights of Women in Africa and AU Youth Charter.
- Supports the Joint EU-AU Strategy and the priorities agreed at the EU-AU Summit.
