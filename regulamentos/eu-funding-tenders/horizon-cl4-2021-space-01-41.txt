Key Objectives
Projects should enhance quality and efficiency of service evolution by addressing policy requirements and technological developments. Expected outcomes include developing efficient product chains through advanced data fusion and processing, plus new algorithms supporting emerging satellite observation data sources.

The initiative targets coupled data assimilation methods for climate reanalysis and multi-model product generation to improve climate prediction realism. Projects must achieve Technology Readiness Level 5-6 by completion and demonstrate integration feasibility with existing services.

Scope and Requirements
This action addresses the evolution of the Copernicus Climate Change Service (C3S), one of six Copernicus services operated by ECMWF under contract with the European Commission. The service provides authoritative information about past, present and future climate, supporting climate adaptation and mitigation policies across Europe.

Expected activities include development of improved seasonal prediction systems and decadal prediction capabilities, enhanced quality control and monitoring of the climate data record archive, and improved management of user interface and access services.

Projects must demonstrate capability to handle multi-petabyte climate datasets and implement efficient dissemination mechanisms. Collaboration with relevant national meteorological services and climate research institutions is expected.
