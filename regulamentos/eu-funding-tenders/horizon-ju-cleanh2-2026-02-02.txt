Demonstrating in-line inspection (ILI) to monitor cracks assuring compatibility for operation with hydrogen in new and re-purposed offshore natural gas pipelines

KEYWORDS
Hydrogen; in-line inspection; natural gas pipelines; offshore pipelines

DESCRIPTION
Current natural gas grids are expected to form a substantial part of future hydrogen transport systems. Offshore pipelines have specific design requirements and cannot be excavated for maintenance like onshore pipes, necessitating longer-lasting infrastructure. Hydrogen's detrimental effects on fracture toughness and ductility, plus increased fatigue crack growth rates, present critical challenges, especially at stress concentration areas like welds.

This topic addresses developing and validating In-Line Inspection tools capable of detecting crack-like defects in offshore hydrogen pipelines. The project must achieve safe operation of repurposed and new offshore pipelines with hydrogen and improve ILI efficiency in stress concentration areas.

EXPECTED OUTCOMES
- Safe operation of repurposed and new offshore hydrogen pipelines
- European technology leadership maintenance in ILI-systems
- Enhanced ILI efficiency for stress concentration areas and crack detection
- Extended pipeline lifetime and reduced operational costs
- Standardisation support for gas grid operations

SCOPE
Projects must demonstrate ILI tools qualifying existing natural gas pipelines for hydrogen service in future offshore infrastructure. Proposals should develop acceptance criteria for defects considering type, size, loading conditions, and hydrogen environment. Tools must detect or monitor crack-type defects with satisfactory resolution through in-line inspection, reaching TRL 6 or higher. Validation through test-runs in high-pressure pipelines is required. Minimum detection performance includes identifying internal surface-breaking circumferential flaws and surface defects not exceeding 3x20 mm.

Consortia must include at least one ILI tool provider and one gas transport grid operator.

TECHNOLOGY READINESS LEVEL
Activities expected to progress from TRL 4 to TRL 6 by project completion.
