Understanding of sex and/or gender-specific mechanisms of cardiovascular diseases: determinants, risk factors and pathways

Description:
This initiative seeks proposals addressing sex and gender-specific health determinants, risk factors and pathways for cardiovascular diseases. CVDs represent the leading cause of premature EU mortality, accounting for approximately 32% of all deaths. The programme emphasises how biological, behavioural, and sociocultural factors create distinct health presentations between genders.

Key research objectives include developing tailored risk models for prevention, detection and diagnostic strategies and identifying novel health determinants through interdisciplinary approaches. Projects should integrate existing health registries and consider life-stage variations alongside genetic, hormonal, psychosocial, and socioeconomic factors.

Scope: Eligible projects are expected to (a) generate evidence on biological mechanisms (hormonal, genetic, epigenetic, immunological) that explain sex differences in CVD onset, progression and outcomes; (b) characterise gender-specific behavioural, psychosocial and socioeconomic risk factors and care-seeking pathways; (c) leverage existing population cohorts, biobanks and electronic health records, with appropriate harmonisation and federated analysis approaches; (d) co-develop sex- and gender-aware risk prediction tools, screening protocols and clinical guidelines; (e) actively involve patient representatives, clinicians and public health stakeholders.
