DESCRIPTION
Projects must advance understanding of anthropogenic climate change's role in extreme weather frequency and intensity. The initiative emphasizes developing improved attribution methodologies, creating pilot event databases, and operationalizing findings for policy, disaster preparedness, and adaptation planning.

EXPECTED OUTCOMES
- Enhanced comprehension of climate change causality in extreme events and cascading impacts
- Better attribution tools and methodologies
- New or improved global extreme event databases
- Knowledge on applying attribution science operationally for civil protection and humanitarian planning

SCOPE
Actions must address: physical climate attribution combining observations and models; natural variability versus anthropogenic drivers; observation collection for robust datasets; compound and cascading event impacts; separation of climate trends from exposure/vulnerability shifts; model enhancement investigations; improved extreme event forecasting; operationalization through co-design with stakeholders; integration of climate justice considerations.

Open science practices required; transparency in models extending beyond documentation to code and data management.
