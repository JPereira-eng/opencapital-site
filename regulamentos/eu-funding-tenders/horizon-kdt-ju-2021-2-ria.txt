Key Research Areas
The call covers 25+ technology domains including:
- Artificial intelligence and machine learning for embedded systems
- Cyber-physical systems and real-time computing
- Embedded systems and edge computing
- Photonics and related technologies
- Robotics and autonomous systems
- Smart manufacturing and Industry 4.0
- Integrated circuits and semiconductor technology
- Secure and dependable systems
- Advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and automotive electronics
- Healthcare electronics and medical devices
- Energy-efficient processing and green computing
- Wireless and wired communication technologies

Objectives
The KDT JU aims to strengthen European industrial leadership in Key Digital Technologies through collaborative R&I projects that bring together industry, research organisations, and academia. Projects should address cross-sectoral technology development with clear industrial relevance and pathway to commercialisation.

Activities supported include research into new computing architectures, development of advanced semiconductor processes and design methodologies, creation of next-generation embedded systems, and innovation in electronic components for strategic application domains.

Eligibility and Conditions
Minimum consortium of 3 independent legal entities from 3 different EU Member States or Associated Countries. Applicants must consult the KDT JU Work Programme 2021 for detailed funding rates. National co-funding from member states complements EU contribution. Large enterprises have specific funding rates not integrated in standard budget tables - separate Excel file uploads required.
