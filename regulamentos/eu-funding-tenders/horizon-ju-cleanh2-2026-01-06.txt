DESCRIPTION
This topic addresses climate emergency and energy crisis by developing renewable hydrogen production through direct solar conversion. It focuses on two pathways: thermochemical cycles (TCC) and photoelectrochemical/photocatalytic (PEC/PC) water splitting using sunlight. The challenge involves improving solar-to-hydrogen conversion efficiency to compete economically while discovering novel, cost-effective catalysts and redox materials. TCC processes use high-temperature solar heat to drive redox reactions decomposing water through chemical steps, currently at early development stages. PEC/PC processes utilize light energy to produce hydrogen from water, offering potential for standalone systems without grid connection. Both approaches require innovations in reactor design, material development, and system integration targeting demonstration at Technology Readiness Level 5.

EXPECTED OUTCOMES
Solar-driven water to hydrogen conversion will be demonstrated in relevant scale and over substantial demonstration periods using innovative reactor concepts. The project aims to develop systems requiring minimal critical raw materials, optimize continuous operation rather than batch processing, develop multiscale models, provide techno-economic and environmental analysis, and identify cost-effective technologies for industrialization after 2030.

Key Performance Indicators:
- Hydrogen production rates exceeding 0.75 kg/m2/year
- Solar-to-hydrogen conversion efficiency: TCC at or above 20%, PEC/PC at or above 10%
- Less than 10% annual STH performance decrease over 300 hours operation
- Hydrogen production costs below 6 EUR/kg H2

SCOPE
Innovations focus on direct conversion of solar energy into hydrogen, eliminating reliance on intermediate photovoltaic-to-electricity pathways. Proposals must integrate subcomponents (materials, devices, reactors, control systems) into functional, operable systems. Key areas include improved active materials, photon and heat management, enhanced transport processes, heat recovery, and potential co-production of additional products.

Scale-up strategy is critical: 250-500 kW for TCC; minimum 10 kW for PEC/PC. Projects should address operation under transient solar irradiation, degradation mechanisms, smart energy management, and end-of-life recycling strategies. Activities start at TRL 3, targeting TRL 5 completion.

OBJECTIVE
Strengthen European leadership in efficient hydrogen production and create new business models for renewable hydrogen based on direct solar conversion technologies, addressing energy security and climate neutrality goals while reducing dependency on conventional fossil fuel-based hydrogen production methods.
