## Full Description

European building renovation increasingly incorporates circularity strategies to minimize environmental impacts and enhance resource efficiency, responding to frameworks like Level(s) and whole-life carbon models aligned with EN 15978 standards. However, current modeling approaches struggle to capture the full complexity of circular strategies, including material reuse, recycling, and the evolving nature of systems across long lifecycles, limiting assessment reliability.

Projects must develop a comprehensive modeling framework integrating circularity assessment within life cycle assessment using cradle-to-cradle system boundaries. This framework should evaluate both embodied emissions (from materials, products, and construction processes) and operational emissions (from HVAC, domestic hot water, electrical appliances, and lighting) across entire building lifecycles per EN 15978 stages.

Applicants must apply their developed framework to at least three real-life buildings across different climatic regions in different Member States or Associated Countries. These buildings should demonstrate ambitious circularity strategies adopted by their renovation project developers targeting zero whole-life carbon outcomes. Circularity approaches must include reuse and/or recycling, plus at least one additional strategy: reduce, repair, refurbish, remanufacture, or repurpose.

Expected outcomes include demonstration of measurable reductions in material flows, life cycle energy use and GHG emissions in renovations integrating circularity, contributing to net-zero whole-life carbon building achievements. Projects should deliver a more robust approach to current modelling frameworks to comprehensively reflect the complexity of circularity strategies in life cycle assessments at building scale.

This initiative implements the European Partnership on People-centric sustainable built environment (Built4People). Resulting projects must contribute to Built4People objectives, transfer knowledge to its innovation cluster network, and report findings supporting Built4People key performance indicators monitoring.

Activities should reach Technology Readiness Level 6-8 by project conclusion, per General Annex B specifications. Activities may commence at any initial TRL.
