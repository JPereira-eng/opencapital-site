## Full Title & Description

Topic Title: Multisectoral approach to tackle chronic non-communicable diseases: implementation research maximizing collaboration and coordination with sectors and in settings beyond the healthcare system (GACD).

### Description Summary
This topic supports research enabling the impacts outlined in Tackling diseases and reducing disease burden. The initiative targets implementation research on strategies to tackle the growing burden of NCDs through actions in sectors and settings outside the traditional health system.

The research should deliver evidence accessible to policymakers, stakeholders, and authorities in low-and middle-income countries and disadvantaged populations in high-income countries regarding how to maximise collaboration and coordination with sectors and in settings beyond the healthcare system in the context of Non-Communicable Diseases.

## Expected Outcomes
Successful proposals should achieve:

1. Improved insights and evidence for researchers, policymakers, and stakeholders on maximizing multisectoral collaboration regarding NCDs.
2. Enhanced understanding of how proposed interventions leverage collaborative engagement across education, workplace, environmental, and community settings.
3. Full engagement of communities and relevant stakeholders in implementing interventions that reduce NCD burden through actions outside traditional health facilities.

## Scope

The topic focuses on implementation research addressing NCDs through:

- Setting Diversity: Research in workplaces, schools, faith-based communities, recreation facilities, prisons, community spaces, and other non-healthcare settings.
- Geographic Focus: Low-and middle-income countries or underserved populations in high-income countries.
- Intervention Types: Evidence-based interventions including policy introduction, screening strategies, community prevention, treatment management, and digital solutions.

Key scope limitations: The topic explicitly excludes standalone feasibility studies, epidemiological cohorts, etiological research, and clinical trials focused solely on intervention efficacy.

## Expected Research Activities

Proposals must address all following components:

1. Methodology: Clear description of implementation research design with statistical rationale, implementation strategies tailored to context, and relevant theoretical frameworks.
2. Outcome Measurement: Appropriate strategies for measuring implementation outcomes and real-world effectiveness indicators.
3. Equity Focus: Specific strategies ensuring equitable implementation reaching vulnerable populations.
4. Team Expertise: Multidisciplinary research teams demonstrating equitable partnership and shared leadership between HIC-LMIC members.
5. Stakeholder Engagement: Evidence of stakeholder support and sustainable intervention pathways beyond project funding.
6. Capacity Building: Opportunities for early-career researcher development in lower-resourced settings.
7. Early Career Involvement: At least one early-career co-investigator required.
