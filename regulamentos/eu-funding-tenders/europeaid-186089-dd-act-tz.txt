EuropeAid/186089/DD/ACT/TZ - Tanzania: Civil Society for Risk Mitigation and Dialogue (EU-Tanzania Economic Cooperation)

Title: Strengthening Civil Society for Risk Mitigation and Dialogue in Support of EU-Tanzania Economic Cooperation
Reference: EuropeAid/186089/DD/ACT/TZ
Programme: NDICI Global Europe - EU-Tanzania Civil Society Programme 2021-2027 / EU External Action - PROSPECT (RELEX-PROSPECT)
Source: fundsforNGOs aggregator and EU-Tanzania bilateral programme documentation
URL of original call page: https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/prospect/external/publishedcalls.htm?callId=186089

OVERVIEW
This EU call for proposals strengthens Tanzanian civil society organisations as independent actors in governance and economic development, supporting their role in advocating for inclusive EU-Tanzania economic cooperation under the EU Global Gateway strategy. The call is part of the EU-Tanzania Civil Society Programme covering 2021-2027 with a total programme budget of EUR 5,757,000. CSOs are positioned as advocates, monitors and facilitators of dialogue between government, communities and private sector actors.

BUDGET
- Total programme budget: EUR 5,757,000 (2021-2027 envelope).
- The EuropeAid/186089 call covers a tranche of this envelope; the specific call envelope is to be confirmed via PROSPECT.
- EU contribution: typical NDICI thematic CSO co-financing rate of up to 90 to 95 per cent.

PROGRAMME PERIOD
2021-2027.

ELIGIBILITY
Eligible applicants:
- Non-profit legal entities including civil society organisations (CSOs), non-governmental organisations (NGOs).
- Entities established in the EU, Tanzania, or other NDICI Global Europe eligible countries.
- Organisations may apply individually or in partnership with co-applicants meeting all criteria.

Specific encouragement for:
- Tanzanian-led organisations and grassroots networks.
- Women-led and youth-led organisations.
- Organisations with operational presence in target regions (mining areas, agricultural zones, Zanzibar, urban centres).

PARTNER REQUIREMENTS
- Co-applicant participation encouraged for thematic complementarity (legal expertise, sectoral knowledge, geographic coverage).
- Partnerships with private sector actors are foreseen for Lots focused on value chains.
- Coordination with national umbrella networks (e.g. Tanzania Association of Non-Governmental Organisations - TANGO) is encouraged.

THEMATIC LOTS / FOCUS AREAS
The EU-Tanzania programme supports four thematic streams:

Lot 1 - Ethical Critical Minerals Value Chains:
- Lithium, nickel, graphite and cobalt with emphasis on environmental, social and governance (ESG) standards.
- Geographic focus: mining regions of Tanzania.

Lot 2 - Digital Civic Space:
- Inclusive, secure digital environments.
- Focus on data governance, cybersecurity, digital rights, and online civic engagement.

Lot 3 - Agricultural Value Chains:
- Coffee, cashew nuts and rice production.
- Market access, smallholder integration, sustainable agricultural practices.

Lot 4 - Structured Thematic Dialogues:
- Multi-stakeholder discussions aligned with EU Global Gateway priorities.
- Convening platforms between government, CSOs and private sector.

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES
- Advocacy on policy reforms in mining, agriculture, digital sectors.
- Monitoring of public investments and ESG compliance in critical minerals supply chains.
- Capacity building for CSOs to engage in policy dialogue.
- Awareness raising and citizen engagement.
- Sub-granting to community-based organisations.
- Research, evidence generation and policy briefs.
- Multi-stakeholder dialogue convening.
- Digital civic space tools (platforms, training, protection of digital rights defenders).
- Value chain support (smallholder cooperatives, market linkages, traceability systems).

ELIGIBLE COSTS
Standard NDICI / EU external action cost categories apply:
- Personnel (Tanzanian and international).
- Travel within Tanzania and to relevant fora.
- Equipment and supplies (limited).
- Sub-grants where allowed.
- Local office and operational running costs.
- Audit, evaluation, communication and visibility.
- Indirect costs at standard flat rate (up to 7 per cent of direct eligible costs).

DEADLINE
7 May 2026 (Brussels time).

EVALUATION CRITERIA
Assessment focuses on:
- Relevance to call objectives and chosen Lot.
- Impact on Tanzanian CSOs and target communities.
- Feasibility of the work plan and partnerships.
- Inclusivity (gender, youth, marginalised groups, geographic balance).
- Alignment with programme objectives and EU Global Gateway priorities.
- Sustainability beyond EU funding.
- Financial management and governance capacity of the consortium.
- Operational track record in Tanzania or comparable contexts.

GEOGRAPHIC FOCUS
Activities target:
- Mining regions for the critical minerals value chain Lot.
- Urban centres for digital civic space.
- Nationwide scope for structured dialogues.
- Specific reference to Zanzibar inclusion depending on thematic area.
- Major agricultural production areas for the agricultural value chain Lot.

PROGRAMME CONTEXT
- Aligned with EU Global Gateway strategy and the EU's strategy for Sub-Saharan Africa.
- Complements the EU-Tanzania Multi-Annual Indicative Programme 2021-2027.
- Supports the Critical Raw Materials Act and the EU's diversification of critical minerals supply chains.
- Aligned with Tanzania Vision 2050 and the country's Five Year Development Plan.
- Coordinated with World Bank, African Development Bank and bilateral donors active in Tanzania.
