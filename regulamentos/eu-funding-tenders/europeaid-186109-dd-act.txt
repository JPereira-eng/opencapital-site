OVERVIEW
This EU call for action grants supports the modernisation of El Salvador's civil registration ecosystem through a comprehensive digital transformation initiative. The call is structured under the Twinning instrument and aims to deliver a secure, human-centred digital civil registration system that strengthens citizens' access to their fundamental rights and to government services. The initiative complements the broader Technical Assistance for the Digital Civil Registry System of El Salvador, launched in March 2026 with a three-year duration.

BUDGET AND DURATION
- Total budget: EUR 1,000,000 (indicative envelope).
- Maximum project duration: 14 months.
- The EUR 1 million figure represents the total EU envelope allocated to this Twinning action; co-financing arrangements follow standard Twinning rules.

ELIGIBLE APPLICANTS
- Restricted to public administrations or entities mandated by EU Member States to act on their behalf in international cooperation (Twinning operator status).
- Applications must be submitted through EU Member States' National Contact Points for Twinning.
- Applicants must follow the rules and procedures of the Twinning Manual published by the European Commission Directorate-General for International Partnerships (INTPA) and Directorate-General for Neighbourhood and Enlargement Negotiations (DG NEAR).
- Private companies, non-governmental organisations and individuals are not eligible as lead applicants under the Twinning instrument.

PARTNER REQUIREMENTS
- Twinning consortia formed by accredited Member State public-sector providers.
- Coordination with the National Contact Points of the participating Member States is mandatory.
- Salvadoran beneficiary institution to be defined in the call documentation (typically the Salvadoran Ministry of Justice and Public Security or the National Centre of Registers, depending on Twinning fiche).

COMPONENTS AND ACTIVITIES
Component 1 - Legal and Institutional Framework:
- Establish a secure, human-centred digital civil registration system.
- Strengthen regulatory and institutional frameworks governing civil registration.
- Ensure compliance with international and EU digital transformation standards (data protection, e-IDAS-equivalent, GDPR-aligned principles).
- Update legal instruments enabling digital identity issuance and recognition.

Component 2 - Operational and Technical Capacity:
- Enhance technological infrastructure, interoperability and information security.
- Build institutional capacities for sustainable adoption (training, change management, governance models).
- Promote citizen engagement and accessibility (multi-channel access, inclusion of vulnerable populations).
- Pilot integration with related government registries (tax ID, social security, health, education) to enable a single digital identity layer.

ELIGIBLE COSTS
Twinning standard cost categories apply:
- Project leader and resident twinning advisor (RTA) fees and per diems.
- Short and medium term experts mission costs (travel, per diems, fees).
- Mandatory result components (training, study visits, equipment supply where allowed).
- Component-specific costs (translation, interpretation, software adaptation).
- Administrative flat rate (typically 6 per cent of operational costs).
The Twinning Manual provides the complete eligible cost breakdown and unit cost ceilings.

DEADLINE
21 April 2026 (Brussels time, indicative based on aggregator data; applicants must verify on the PROSPECT call page).

EVALUATION CRITERIA
1. Alignment with project objectives and El Salvador's civil registration priorities.
2. Strength and feasibility of proposed solutions, methodology and work plan.
3. Institutional implementation capacity of the proposing Member State administration.
4. Long-term sustainability and ecosystem benefits, including ownership by the Salvadoran beneficiary.
5. Quality of risk analysis, monitoring and evaluation framework.
6. Cost effectiveness and budget realism.

EVALUATION PROCESS
Standard Twinning two-stage evaluation: pre-selection by the European Commission services, followed by selection committee with the beneficiary institution and Member State Twinning experts.

PROGRAMME CONTEXT
- The action is part of the EU's broader engagement with El Salvador on digital governance, rule of law and inclusive public services.
- Complements the Team Europe Initiative for digital transformation in Central America.
- Aligned with El Salvador's national digital agenda and the modernisation of identity infrastructure (DUI, single digital identity).
