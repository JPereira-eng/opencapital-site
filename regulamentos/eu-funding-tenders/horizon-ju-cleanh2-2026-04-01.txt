DESCRIPTION AND EXPECTED OUTCOMES
The topic addresses development of reversible proton conducting ceramics (PCC) technology capable of producing hydrogen and operating with hydrogenated fuels. Key expected outcomes include:

- Development enabling replacement of energy/emission-intensive thermal processes by electrochemical ones
- Integration with renewable energy sources validating dynamic performance
- Reduction of capital and operational expenditure through innovation
- Evaluation of new business models benefiting from reversible or multi-mode operation
- Contribution to European leadership in reversible PCC technology with European supply chain

Target KPIs (2030):
- Cell current density: above 0.75 A/cm2
- Cell degradation: below 1% per 1,000 hours
- Stack current density: above 0.5 A/cm2
- Stack degradation: below 2.5% per 1,000 hours
- Faradaic efficiency: at or above 84% in electrolysis mode

SCOPE
Projects should focus on development and validation of novel proton ceramic or co-ionic electrochemical cells and stacks, which operate reversibly in electrolysis and fuel cell mode with high efficiency and durability.

Key Requirements:
- Demonstrations of reversible operation in both fuel cell and electrolysis modes
- Minimum 2,000-hour operation testing at representative conditions
- Stack production with multiple repeating units
- Minimum 1 kW power class demonstration
- Techno-economic assessment of reversible technology
- Evaluation of at least two use cases with environmental life cycle analysis
- Material sustainability assessment addressing critical and strategic raw materials

Technology Readiness Level Expected: TRL 4
