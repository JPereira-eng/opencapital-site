# Expression of Interest: Cross-Cultural Conversations on Climate Leadership

## Full Name

Expression of Interest: Cross-Cultural Conversations on Climate Leadership

## Programme Code

No specific programme code is provided in the call documentation.

## Description

Climate KIC is organizing facilitated conversations between climate-focused entrepreneurs and founders based in Asia, Oceania, and Europe. The initiative represents a 2026 partnership with NO.17 Foundation and TPC Group (Tsao Pao Chee) to advance well-being economy principles. According to the materials, "Each conversation is a 45-minute facilitated bilateral exchange, pairing one entrepreneur based in Asia or Oceania with one based in Europe, creating a structured space for peer reflection and the kind of cross-cultural exchange that rarely happens in formal settings."

The conversations aim to explore "the inner capacities, mindsets and skills that enable people to act with resilience and purpose." Recordings will be organized to map "developed and underdeveloped leadership capabilities across different contexts."

## Objectives

The initiative addresses fragmentation within climate entrepreneurship ecosystems across regions. As stated in the materials, "these efforts often remain fragmented and disconnected from one another. Opportunities for cross-border learning and alignment are scarce, and the well-being economy discourse frequently remains too abstract to be actionable."

Core objectives include:

- Creating structured peer reflection opportunities between entrepreneurs from different continents
- Facilitating cross-cultural exchange around regenerative entrepreneurship
- Mapping leadership capabilities and inner capacities needed for climate work
- Advancing understanding of well-being economy principles in practice
- Generating insights on how business models can embody people-first and planet-first values
- Building connection between fragmented entrepreneurial efforts globally

Sample conversation themes cover four areas: people and agency (examining how ventures have changed entrepreneurs' relationships to "impact, purpose and resilience"); business model and value ("what does it mean in practice to put people and planet first"); systems and place (understanding engagement with "communities, supply chains, ecosystems, institutions"); and cross-regional exchange (learning what "viable futures for people and planet" means across different contexts).

## Eligible Beneficiaries

The call welcomes professionals meeting specific criteria:

Role Requirements: Applicants must be "an entrepreneur, founder or co-founder of a start-up, scale-up or SME."

Geographic Scope: The materials indicate "You are based in Asia, Oceania, or Europe (applicants from all regions are welcome)."

Thematic Focus: Participants should operate "in climate innovation, circular economy, sustainability, or a related field."

Practical Requirements: Applicants must be "available for a 45-minute online conversation between June and August 2026" and be "comfortable having a facilitated conversation in English. Where possible, we will match participants who share the same native language."

Consent: Participants "must consent to having the conversation recorded for research purposes."

The call particularly targets "people who are already thinking about, or actively navigating, the tensions between growth, impact and systemic change."

## Funding Amount and Grant Details

Two participants will receive travel grants supporting attendance at AT ONE Impact Week 2026, occurring September 8-12 at Suntec Singapore Convention & Exhibition Centre.

The grant explicitly covers contributions toward "the cost of return flights, accommodation, and event registration." However, recipients must cover their own "travel documentation, including visas, travel insurance and any associated fees" as these are "not covered by the grant."

The materials note that "Winners will be notified with sufficient lead time to make the necessary arrangements."

Attending Impact Week provides access to "high-level convenings on place-based systems transformation, regenerative entrepreneurship, and cross-regional collaboration," "direct connections with the Climate KIC network, advisors, and funders active across Asia and Europe," and "the opportunity to showcase your own work and story on an international stage."

## Selection Criteria

All participants automatically qualify for grant consideration. Grant winners are selected based on responses to Section 3 of the application form, evaluated against three criteria:

1. Demonstrated Embodiment of Well-Being Economy Principles (Question 1)
Reviewers assess whether "the applicant show a concrete, specific way their venture puts people and planet ahead of conventional growth metrics?" The documentation emphasizes that "Reviewers will look for real examples over abstract statements, how decisions are made, what is measured, and what has been sacrificed or redesigned in service of this commitment."

2. Depth of Personal and Entrepreneurial Reflection (Question 2)
The assessment examines whether applicants demonstrate "genuine self-awareness about the inner demands of building a regenerative business." Strong responses "will go beyond generic challenges and name a specific capacity, such as holding uncertainty, sustaining purpose under pressure, or navigating systemic resistance, with honest reflection on how it has shaped their leadership."

3. Quality and Curiosity of Cross-Regional Learning Intent (Question 3)
Evaluators examine whether applicants "articulate a specific, genuine learning question for a entrepreneur from another region?" The documentation favors "responses that reveal intellectual curiosity, humility, and a clear sense of what the applicant does not yet know, rather than a desire to share their own work."

## Additional Context

This initiative emerges from Climate KIC's 2026 partnership advancing "the vision of a 'well-being economy.'" A well-being economy is described as one "designed to put people and planet over profit. It measures progress through diverse lenses, from health and social connection to sustainability, equity, education and economic resilience."

The call emphasizes that participation is grounded in "Climate KIC's vision: a just, climate-resilient, beautiful future for all."
