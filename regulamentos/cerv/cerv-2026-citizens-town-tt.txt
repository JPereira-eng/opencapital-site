CERV-2026-CITIZENS-TOWN-TT
Town Twinning - Call for Proposals
Citizens, Equality, Rights and Values (CERV) Programme

PROGRAMME: CERV - Citizens, Equality, Rights and Values
CALL IDENTIFIER: CERV-2026-CITIZENS-TOWN-TT
MANAGING AUTHORITY: European Education and Culture Executive Agency (EACEA)
LEGAL BASE: Regulation (EU) 2021/692 of the European Parliament and of the Council

OBJECTIVE
The Town Twinning action under CERV aims to strengthen the links between municipalities and local communities across Europe by supporting direct people-to-people exchanges. The objective is to foster mutual understanding, solidarity and a sense of belonging to a common European space by bringing together citizens of different nationalities and backgrounds.

BUDGET AND FUNDING
Total indicative call budget: EUR 6,000,000
Minimum grant per project: EUR 5,000
Maximum grant per project: EUR 25,000
Co-financing rate: up to 80% of eligible costs

DEADLINE
Submission deadline: 23 September 2026

ELIGIBLE APPLICANTS
- Cities and municipalities from eligible CERV countries
- Twinning committees or associations representing municipalities
- Associations of local authorities involved in town twinning activities

Partnership requirements: The application must involve at least one partner municipality or twinning committee from another eligible country. At least one applicant must be from an EU Member State.

ELIGIBLE COUNTRIES
All EU Member States plus CERV programme associated countries (Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Kosovo, Moldova, Montenegro, North Macedonia, Serbia, Ukraine).

ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES
- Exchange visits between twinned towns (citizens, young people, elected representatives, civil society)
- Citizen meetings on EU topics: democracy, common values, European history and cultural heritage
- Events celebrating Europe Day (9 May) and other EU commemorative dates
- Activities promoting engagement in local democracy
- Cross-border cooperation projects with counterpart municipalities
- Activities addressing shared local challenges (environment, social inclusion, digital transition)
- Cultural, educational and social exchanges between citizens

INELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES
- Activities that are primarily commercial or profit-making in nature
- Activities that are primarily political or lobbying in nature
- Activities limited to one country (must have cross-border dimension)
- Conferences or seminars not directly linked to twinning activities

ELIGIBLE COSTS
- Travel and accommodation for participants in cross-border exchanges
- Interpretation and translation costs directly linked to the project
- Organisation costs for events and activities
- Communication and dissemination costs
- Coordination costs between partner municipalities

AWARD CRITERIA
- Relevance: alignment with CERV objectives and democratic participation (30 points)
- Quality: soundness of methodology and project design (30 points)
- Impact: expected results and sustainability (20 points)
- Quality of the team and partnership (20 points)
- Overall threshold: 70 points out of 100

PROJECT DURATION
Minimum: 6 months
Maximum: 18 months

HOW TO APPLY
Applications must be submitted via the EU Funding and Tenders Portal (F&T Portal): https://ec.europa.eu/info/funding-tenders/opportunities/portal/
Prior registration in the Participant Register is required. Each applicant must have a valid Participant Identification Code (PIC).
