name: Editorial article structure (h2s and eyebrows)
description: Open Capital editorial articles must use h2s and eyebrows sparingly, with natural editorial voice, not as chapter headings or category labels on every section
type: feedback
---

Editorial articles published in `conhecimento/` (informativo, trend, opiniao, youtube skills) must follow strict structure rules:

- **H2 cap by length**: max 1 h2 per ~600-800 words. Article of 3000 words → 4-5 h2s, never 8-9.
- **Eyebrow scarcity**: max 1-2 eyebrows in the entire article. Mandatory only on the closing "Perspetiva Open Capital" section. Optional on the first section if it serves as a category label. Nowhere else.
- **H2 voice prohibitions**: no "X que Y" formula ("A empresa que construiu...", "A juíza que decidiu..."), no listicle patterns ("Três razões...", "Quatro decisões..."), no overly descriptive labels, no announce-what's-coming headings.
- **H2 voice preferences**: short affirmative phrases, evocative single nouns ("A escalada", "A ressaca"), direct questions, tension/contradiction phrases.
- **Alternative ways to break flow**: art-divider, pull-quote, paragraph break, art-highlight. Not every pivot needs a heading.

**Why:** in 2026-04-25, user analyzed published articles and found 7-9 eyebrow+h2 combos per article (one every 300-400 words). The eyebrow had become a mini-headline competing with h2 ("O caso", "A escalada", "O epicentro"), the h2s repeated formulas, and the uniform rhythm read as mechanical/corporate rather than premium editorial. The 4 editorial skills had no frequency caps and demonstrated eyebrow+h2 as the canonical example, so the model defaulted to using the combo on every section.

**How to apply:** when writing or reviewing any editorial article for opencapital-site (informativo, trend, opiniao, youtube series), explicitly count h2s against the cap and check eyebrow placement. Read the h2s as a list and look for the prohibited patterns. If a section pivot can survive without a heading, remove the heading. Rule of thumb: if in doubt whether there's one h2 too many, there is.
